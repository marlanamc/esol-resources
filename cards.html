VERB

Action or state.

  • V1 / V1-3rd alone - eat/eats, work/works
  • V2 alone - ate, worked, went
  • Helper + Verb - do/does/did/will + eat
  • To Be + V-ing - am/is/are + eating
  • Have/Has/Had + V3 - has + eaten
  • Have/Has/Had + been + V-ing - has been eating

Example: eats, is studying, will go

WHO

Subject of the sentence.

  • Pronoun - I, you, he, she, they, we
  • Possessive + Noun - my friend, your teacher, his sister
  • Article + Noun - a student, an apple, the teacher
  • Noun - John, Maria, students

Example: She, My friend, The students

WHEN

Time (beginning or end of sentence).

  • Time word - now, today, yesterday
  • Time phrase - every day, last night
  • Preposition + Time - at 3pm, on Monday

Example: Yesterday, I studied English.

Example: I studied English yesterday.

WHAT

Object or thing.

  • Noun - pizza, book, homework
  • Determiner + Noun - a book, the pizza
  • Pronoun - me, you, him, them

Example: pizza, the keys, them

, Comma

Use a comma to:

  • Separate items in a list (apples, oranges, bananas)
  • Join sentences with connectors (I ate pizza, and I drank tea)
  • Add extra info (My friend, Maria, lives here)
  • After WHEN or WHERE at the start (When it rains, I stay home)

WHERE

Place (usually after WHAT or at end).

  • Preposition + Noun - at home, in the park
  • Preposition + Proper Noun - in Boston, at Starbucks
  • Adverb - here, there, everywhere

Example: At the park, we played soccer.

Example: We played soccer at the park.

? Question Mark

Use a question mark at the end of questions.

  • Yes/No Questions - start with helping verb (Did you go? Are you ready?)
  • WH- Questions - start with question word (Who came? What happened?)

Example: Did you buy groceries yesterday?

Example: Where did you meet your friend?

Tip: Questions end with ? not .

QUESTION WORD

Question words start questions and replace WHO/WHAT/WHERE/WHEN.

  • Who - asks about a person
  • What - asks about a thing
  • Where - asks about a place
  • When - asks about time
  • Why - asks about reason
  • How - asks about manner

Example: Who did you meet? What did you buy? Where did you go?

Tip: Question word comes first, then the verb!
