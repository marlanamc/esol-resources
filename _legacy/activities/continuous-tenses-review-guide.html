‚ö†Ô∏è Common Confusions to Avoid

‚ùå Don't Use Continuous with State Verbs!

State verbs describe states, feelings, thoughts, and senses - they are NOT actions. You CANNOT use continuous forms with them!

Common state verbs: like, love, hate, want, need, know, understand, believe, remember, forget, see, hear, smell, taste, feel (emotion), be, have (possess), own, belong

‚ùå Wrong: "I am liking pizza" | "She is knowing the answer" | "They are wanting coffee"

‚úÖ Correct: "I like pizza" | "She knows the answer" | "They want coffee"

üí° Continuous vs Simple Tenses:
  • Continuous: Actions happening NOW, temporarily, or at a specific time ‚Üí "I am studying" (right now)
  • Simple: Habits, routines, facts, permanent situations ‚Üí "I study every day" (habit)
  • Remember: Continuous = temporary/in progress | Simple = permanent/habitual
üí° Present Continuous for Future:

Present Continuous can be used for planned future arrangements (like appointments or scheduled events).

Example: "I'm meeting John tomorrow" (we have a plan/arrangement)

This is different from Future Continuous, which describes what will be happening at a specific future time.

üí° Past Continuous + Past Simple:

When one action interrupts another, use Past Continuous for the longer action and Past Simple for the interruption.

Example: "I was studying when the phone rang." (studying = longer action, rang = interruption)

Quick Reference: The Three Continuous Tenses

Present Continuous

When to use: Actions happening now, temporary situations, future arrangements

Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing
I: am | He/She/It: is | You/We/They: are
Examples:
I am studying right now.
She is working at the moment.
They aren't sleeping now.
Are you coming to the party tomorrow? (future arrangement)

Time expressions: now, right now, at the moment, currently, today, this week, tonight, tomorrow (arrangements)

Past Continuous

When to use: Actions in progress at a specific time in the past, interrupted actions, parallel actions

Subject + was/were + verb-ing
I/He/She/It: was | You/We/They: were
Examples:
I was studying at 3 PM yesterday.
She was working when I called.
They weren't sleeping at midnight.
Were you watching TV when I arrived?

Time expressions: at 3 PM yesterday, while, when, during, at that time, all morning/afternoon

Future Continuous

When to use: Actions that will be in progress at a specific future time

Subject + will be + verb-ing
All subjects use will be/won't be
Examples:
I will be studying at 3 PM tomorrow.
She will be working when you arrive.
They won't be sleeping at midnight.
Will you be waiting for me?

Time expressions: at 3 PM tomorrow, when you arrive, at that time, this time next week

üí° Key Tip: Look at the time expressions and context! Continuous tenses show actions in progress at a specific time. Also remember: NO continuous forms with state verbs!

  • ‚úÖ Past Continuous: Actions in progress at specific past time OR interrupted actions ‚Üí Look for: at 3 PM yesterday, when, while, was/were + verb-ing
