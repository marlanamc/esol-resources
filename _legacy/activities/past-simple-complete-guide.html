What is Past Simple? It's used to talk about completed actions in the past, things that happened at a specific time, and finished events.

When Do We Use Past Simple? Understanding the Meaning

Why is meaning important?

Knowing the structure (how to form sentences) is important, but understanding the meaning (when and why to use Past Simple) helps you use it correctly in real life!

üìå Past Simple is used for:

  • 1. Completed Actions in the Past - Actions that started and finished in the past
    I finished my homework yesterday.
    She visited Paris last summer.
    They moved to a new house in 2020.

    üí° These actions are finished - they happened and are complete.

  • 2. Specific Time in the Past - Actions that happened at a particular time
    I met him at 3 PM yesterday.
    She was born in 1995.
    We started class at 9:00.

    üí° These have a specific time mentioned (yesterday, last week, in 1995, at 3 PM).

  • 3. Past Habits (That Are Finished) - Things you did regularly in the past but don't do now
    I played soccer when I was a child.
    She lived in London for five years.
    They went to the beach every summer.

    üí° These describe habits or situations that happened in the past but are finished now.

  • 4. Sequential Actions - Actions that happened one after another
    I woke up, brushed my teeth, and ate breakfast.
    She opened the door and walked in.

    üí° These actions happened in order, one after another.

  • 5. Past Facts - Things that were true in the past
    The store closed at 6 PM yesterday.
    It rained last night.
    He was a teacher before he retired.

    üí° These describe facts or situations that existed in the past.

    • üí° Key Point:

    Past Simple describes actions that are finished and completed in the past. It's NOT for actions that are still happening or continuing (that's Past Continuous).

    Compare:

    • "I worked at a school" (Past Simple - finished, I don't work there now)
    • "I was working when you called" (Past Continuous - action in progress)

    Note: State verbs (like, know, want, understand) use Past Simple: "I liked that movie" (NOT "I was liking"). State verbs don't use continuous forms!

    Time Expressions - Your Clues for Past Simple!

    Why are time expressions important?

    Time expressions are like clues that help you know which tense to use! When you see these words, they tell you that something happened in the past and is finished.

    üìÖ Time Expressions for Past Simple

    These time expressions help you identify Past Simple. When you see them, think: "This happened in the past and is finished!"

    Common Time Expressions:
    Yesterday
    Last week/month/year
    Two days ago
    In 2020 / In 1995
    At 3 PM yesterday
    This morning (if it's afternoon/evening now)
    When I was a child
    Before
    Examples:
    I finished my homework yesterday.
    She visited Paris last summer.
    They moved here two years ago.
    He was born in 1995.
    We met at 3 PM yesterday.
    I played soccer when I was a child.
    üí° Key Point:

    When you see time expressions like "yesterday", "last week", "ago", "in 2020" ‚Üí think Past Simple!

    Compare:

    • "I worked yesterday" (Past Simple - finished action)
    • "I work every day" (Present Simple - habit)
    • "I will work tomorrow" (Future Simple - future)

    Past Simple - Positive (Affirmative) Sentences

    What are positive sentences?

    Positive sentences tell us what someone did or what something was in the past. They're not negative (saying "no") and they're not questions.

    Subject + past tense verb
    ‚ö†Ô∏è Important: In Past Simple, all subjects use the same past tense form!
    üìù Two Types of Verbs:
    • Regular verbs: Add -ed (work ‚Üí worked, play ‚Üí played)
    • Irregular verbs: Change completely (go ‚Üí went, see ‚Üí saw, do ‚Üí did)
    Look at these examples:
    I worked at a school yesterday. (regular verb: work ‚Üí worked)
    She went to the hospital last week. (irregular verb: go ‚Üí went)
    They lived in Boston in 2020. (regular verb: live ‚Üí lived)
    He saw a movie yesterday. (irregular verb: see ‚Üí saw)
    We liked that restaurant. (regular verb: like ‚Üí liked)
    Maria did her homework. (irregular verb: do ‚Üí did)
    üí° Remember:
    • All subjects (I, you, he, she, it, we, they) use the same past tense form
    • Regular verbs: add -ed (or -d if verb ends in e)
    • Irregular verbs: memorize the past forms (go ‚Üí went, see ‚Üí saw, have ‚Üí had)
    • No need to add -s or -es like in Present Simple!

    Past Simple - Negative Sentences

    What are negative sentences?

    Negative sentences tell us what someone didn't do or what something wasn't in the past. We use didn't (did not) to make negatives.

    Subject + didn't + base verb
    ‚ö†Ô∏è Important: In Past Simple, all subjects use didn't + base verb (no past tense form)!
    Look at these examples:
    I didn't like pizza. (I + didn't + base verb)
    She didn't go to the party. (She + didn't + base verb)
    They didn't work yesterday. (They + didn't + base verb)
    He didn't see the movie. (He + didn't + base verb)
    We didn't speak Spanish. (We + didn't + base verb)
    Maria didn't finish her homework. (Maria + didn't + base verb)
    üí° Remember:
    • All subjects (I, you, he, she, it, we, they) use didn't
    • After didn't, the verb is always in base form (no -ed, no past tense!)
    • Even irregular verbs use base form after didn't (didn't go, didn't see, didn't do)

    Past Simple - Questions

    How do we make questions?

    To make questions, we start with Did, then add the subject, then the base verb.

    Did + subject + base verb ?
    ‚ö†Ô∏è Important: In Past Simple, all subjects use Did + base verb (no past tense form)!
    Look at these examples:
    Did you like pizza? (Did + you + base verb)
    Did she go to the party? (Did + she + base verb)
    Did they work here? (Did + they + base verb)
    Did he see the movie? (Did + he + base verb)
    Did we speak English? (Did + we + base verb)
    Did Maria finish her homework? (Did + Maria + base verb)
    üí° Remember:
    • All subjects (I, you, he, she, it, we, they) start with Did
    • After Did, the verb is always in base form (no -ed, no past tense!)
    • Even irregular verbs use base form after Did (Did you go? Did she see? Did he do?)
    • Don't forget the question mark ?
