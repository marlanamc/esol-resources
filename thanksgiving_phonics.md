# **Thanksgiving Pronunciation Worksheet**

**Focus: TH and F Sounds**
**Plus B/V Review**

---

## **1. Pronunciation Reminder**

### **F sound**

Top teeth touch the bottom lip. Air moves out smoothly.
Examples: **food, fun, fall**

### **TH sound**

Tongue goes gently **between** your teeth.
Two kinds:

* **Voiceless TH**: no vibration (think, thanks, thick)
* **Voiced TH**: vibration in the throat (this, that, those)

### **Quick B / V Review**

* **B**: lips **together**, little burst of air (**baby, brown**)
* **V**: top teeth on bottom lip, **vibration** (**very, visit**)

---

## **2. Reading: “Thankful for Family”**

This week is **Thanksgiving**.
Ana and her **family** get ready for a small dinner with friends.

The **father** cooks fish and vegetables.
The **mother** makes a **thick** pumpkin soup.
Ana helps by cutting fruit for a fresh **fall** salad.

They talk about what they are **thankful** for this year.
Ana is thankful for her **friends**.
Her mother is thankful for good **weather**.
Her father is thankful for **fun** moments with his family.

They smile, eat, and say **thank you** for a warm evening together.

---

## **3. TH vs F Minimal Pairs**

Circle the word you hear your teacher say.

### **A. Voiceless TH and F**

1. **thin / fin**
2. **think / fink**
3. **thick / fick**
4. **thank / sank**
5. **throw / fro**

### **B. Voiced TH and F**

1. **this / vis**
2. **their / fair**
3. **they / vay**
4. **those / foes**
5. **that / fat**

## **4. B/V Review Minimal Pairs**

Circle the word you hear:

1. **berry / very**
2. **boat / vote**
3. **ban / van**
4. **best / vest**
5. **bow / vow**

## **5. Pronunciation Practice Sentences**

Have students read aloud:

1. I am **thankful** for my **family**.
2. The **father** cooks a **thick** fish.
3. **This** food is **very** fresh.
4. Ana can **think** of **five** things she loves.
5. The **fall** **weather** is **warm** this week.
