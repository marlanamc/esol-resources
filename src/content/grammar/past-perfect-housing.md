# Past Perfect Tense - Housing Context

## What is Past Perfect?

**Form:** `had + past participle`

**Use:** To talk about something that happened **before** another past event.

## Housing Examples

### Before Moving In
- "Before I signed the lease, I **had checked** the apartment carefully."
- "When I met the landlord, I **had already researched** tenant rights."
- "She **had saved** $2,000 before she started looking for apartments."

### Problems and Solutions
- "The water heater broke because the owner **hadn't maintained** it properly."
- "By the time I called maintenance, the leak **had already damaged** the floor."
- "I felt relieved because I **had taken** photos of the apartment condition."

### Housing History
- "I **had lived** with roommates for three years before I got my own place."
- "They **had rented** in that neighborhood for ten years before they bought a house."
- "Before this building, we **had stayed** in a smaller apartment."

## Practice Sentences

Complete with past perfect:

1. Before I moved in, the landlord ______ (promise) to fix the kitchen.
2. When I discovered the mold, I ______ (already pay) the security deposit.
3. She didn't sign the lease because she ______ (find) a better option.
4. By the time the inspector arrived, the tenants ______ (already clean) everything.
5. He was nervous because he ______ (never live) alone before.

## Speaking Practice

Use these prompts to practice past perfect:

- "Tell me about a time you had a problem with your apartment. What had happened before you discovered it?"
- "Describe your last move. What had you done before moving day?"
- "Talk about finding your current home. What research had you completed?"

## Signal Words

- **Before**
- **After**
- **By the time**
- **When**
- **Already**
- **Just**
- **Never**

## Common Mistakes

❌ "I have checked the apartment before I signed the lease."
✅ "I **had checked** the apartment before I signed the lease."

❌ "She didn't signed the lease because she found something better."
✅ "She didn't sign the lease because she **had found** something better."
