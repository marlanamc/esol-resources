# Speaking Prompts - Consumer & Finance

## Instructions for Students
1. Read each prompt carefully
2. Take 30 seconds to think about your answer
3. Speak for 1-2 minutes
4. Try to use complete sentences and target vocabulary
5. Record yourself if possible to check your progress

---

## Level 1: Daily Shopping

### Grocery Shopping
- "Describe your typical grocery shopping trip. Where do you go? What do you usually buy?"
- "Tell me about how you save money on groceries. What strategies do you use?"
- "Explain how you plan your meals for the week."

### Personal Shopping
- "Talk about the last thing you bought for yourself. Why did you choose it?"
- "Describe your shopping habits. Do you prefer online or in-store shopping?"
- "Tell me about a time you found a great deal or discount."

---

## Level 2: Banking & Money Management

### Bank Visits
- "Describe what you do when you go to the bank. What services do you use?"
- "Tell me about opening a bank account. What questions did they ask?"
- "Explain how you withdraw money or check your balance."

### Budget Planning
- "How do you keep track of your spending? What methods work for you?"
- "Tell me about creating a budget. What categories do you include?"
- "Describe your monthly expenses. What takes up most of your money?"

---

## Level 3: Past Financial Decisions (Using Past Perfect)

### Before Making Purchases
- "Before you bought your phone, what research had you done?"
- "Tell me about your last major purchase. What had you saved before buying it?"
- "Before you chose your current bank, what options had you considered?"

### Learning from Experience
- "Describe a time you wasted money. What had you not considered before spending?"
- "Tell me about a financial mistake you made. What had you assumed that turned out wrong?"
- "Before you learned to budget, what spending habits had you developed?"

---

## Level 4: Future Financial Plans (Using Future Perfect)

### Savings Goals
- "By the end of this year, how much money will you have saved?"
- "Before next semester starts, what will you have paid off?"
- "By this time next year, what financial goals will you have accomplished?"

### Major Purchases
- "Before you buy a car, what research will you have completed?"
- "By next summer, what will you have saved for a down payment?"
- "Before you move to a new apartment, what expenses will you have planned for?"

---

## Level 5: Complex Financial Situations

### Dealing with Problems
- "You found an error on your bank statement. What do you do? Who do you call?"
- "Your credit card was charged twice for one purchase. How do you handle this?"
- "You need to dispute a bill. What steps do you take?"

### Customer Service Calls
- "Call your phone company to ask about a strange charge. What do you say?"
- "You want to lower your internet bill. How do you negotiate with the company?"
- "Your subscription auto-renewed but you wanted to cancel. What's your conversation?"

### Financial Advice
- "Your friend wants to start saving money. What advice do you give them?"
- "Someone is asking to borrow money. How do you respond?"
- "Explain to a new immigrant how banking works in the U.S."

---

## Role-Play Scenarios

### Calling Customer Service
**You:** "Hello, I'm calling about my recent bill/statement..."
**Problem:** Choose one: incorrect charge, service not working, billing question
**Your task:** Explain the problem clearly and ask for a solution

### Negotiating a Price
**Situation:** You want a discount on a large purchase
**Your task:** Negotiate a better price politely but firmly
**Include:** research you've done, competing prices, what you're willing to pay

### Returning an Item
**Situation:** You want to return something you bought
**Store policy:** 30-day return policy, you have receipt, item is in good condition
**Your task:** Explain why you're returning it and ask for refund or exchange

## Pronunciation Practice

### Key Vocabulary
Practice saying these words aloud:
- Budget / expense
- Discount / sale
- Purchase / transaction
- Receipt / invoice
- Credit / debit
- Withdraw / deposit
- Balance / statement

### Useful Phrases
- "I'm calling about a charge on my bill..."
- "I'd like to dispute this transaction."
- "Can you explain this fee to me?"
- "I was wondering if there's a discount available..."
- "I need to return this item because..."
- "What's your return policy?"

---

## Idioms and Expressions

### Money Idioms
- "I need to tighten my belt" (spend less money)
- "It costs an arm and a leg" (very expensive)
- "I'm saving for a rainy day" (saving for emergencies)
- "Money doesn't grow on trees" (money is limited)
- "I'm broke" (I have no money)

### Shopping Expressions
- "It's a steal" (very good price)
- "It's a rip-off" (too expensive)
- "Shop around" (compare prices at different stores)
- "Bang for your buck" (good value for the price)

## Progress Tracking

### Weekly Goals
- Week 1: Complete 5 Level 1 prompts (daily shopping)
- Week 2: Complete 5 Level 2 prompts (banking & budget)
- Week 3: Complete 5 Level 3 prompts (use past perfect)
- Week 4: Complete 5 Level 4 prompts (use future perfect)
- Week 5: Practice 3 role-play scenarios
- Week 6: Complete 3 Level 5 complex scenarios

### Self-Evaluation
After each prompt, ask yourself:
- ☐ Did I speak for at least 1 minute?
- ☐ Did I use financial vocabulary correctly?
- ☐ Did I use the target grammar (past/future perfect)?
- ☐ Was my pronunciation clear?
- ☐ Could I have this conversation in real life?
