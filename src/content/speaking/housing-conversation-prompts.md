# Speaking Prompts - Housing & Home Life

## Instructions for Students
1. Read each prompt carefully
2. Take 30 seconds to think about your answer
3. Speak for 1-2 minutes
4. Try to use complete sentences
5. Record yourself if possible to check your progress

---

## Level 1: Basic Descriptions

### Your Current Home
- "Describe the place where you live now. How many rooms? What's your favorite room?"
- "Tell me about your neighborhood. What do you like about it?"
- "Describe your bedroom or living room. What furniture do you have?"

### Daily Home Life
- "What do you usually do at home in the evening?"
- "Describe your morning routine at home."
- "Tell me about cooking in your kitchen. What do you usually make?"

---

## Level 2: Problems & Solutions

### Common Housing Problems
- "Tell me about a time something broke in your home. What did you do?"
- "Describe a problem you had with a neighbor. How did you solve it?"
- "Talk about a time you lost your keys. What happened?"

### Calling for Help
- "You need to call your landlord about a broken heater. What will you say?"
- "Your electricity went out. Who do you call? What do you tell them?"
- "You found mold in your bathroom. What should you do? Who do you contact?"

---

## Level 3: Past Experiences (Using Past Perfect)

### Before Moving
- "Before you moved to your current home, where had you lived?"
- "Tell me about your last apartment. What problems had you experienced before you left?"
- "Before you signed your current lease, what had you learned about renting?"

### Housing History
- "Describe the first place you lived alone. What had you done to prepare?"
- "Talk about a time you had to move quickly. What had you packed before the movers arrived?"
- "Before you found your current home, how many places had you looked at?"

---

## Level 4: Future Plans (Using Future Perfect)

### Moving Plans
- "By this time next year, where will you have moved to?"
- "Before you get your own place, what will you have saved?"
- "By next spring, what home improvements will you have completed?"

### Financial Goals
- "By the end of this year, how much money will you have saved for housing?"
- "Before you buy a house, what research will you have completed?"
- "By next month, what bills will you have paid off to improve your budget?"

---

## Level 5: Complex Scenarios

### Tenant Rights
- "Your landlord wants to enter your apartment without notice. What are your rights? What do you say?"
- "Your security deposit wasn't returned. How do you handle this situation?"
- "The rent increased suddenly. What steps do you take?"

### Negotiations
- "You want to get a lower rent price. How do you negotiate with your landlord?"
- "Your roommate isn't paying their share. What conversation do you have?"
- "You need to break your lease early. What do you explain to your landlord?"

### Emergency Situations
- "There's a fire in your building. What do you do? Who do you call?"
- "A pipe burst and is flooding your apartment. What's your immediate response?"
- "Someone is trying to break into your home. What actions do you take?"

---

## Role-Play Scenarios

### Calling Maintenance
**You:** "Hello, this is [your name] from apartment [number]. I'm calling because..."
**Problem:** Choose one: no hot water, broken AC, leaking faucet, broken lock
**Your task:** Explain the problem clearly and ask when it can be fixed

### Talking to Landlord
**Situation:** You want to make improvements to your apartment
**Your task:** Ask permission and explain who will pay for the changes
**Examples:** paint walls, install shelves, get a pet, add a security camera

### Complaining About Noise
**Situation:** Your neighbors are very loud late at night
**Your task:** Talk to your landlord about this problem
**Include:** when it happens, how it affects you, what you want them to do

## Pronunciation Practice

### Key Vocabulary
Practice saying these words aloud:
- Landlord / tenant
- Lease / rental agreement
- Security deposit
- Maintenance / repair
- Neighborhood
- Utilities
- Eviction / notice

### Useful Phrases
- "I'm calling about..."
- "I need to report a problem with..."
- "When can you fix this?"
- "Is this covered by my lease?"
- "I'd like to make a complaint about..."
- "What are my rights regarding...?"

---

## Progress Tracking

### Weekly Goals
- Week 1: Complete 5 Level 1 prompts
- Week 2: Complete 5 Level 2 prompts  
- Week 3: Complete 5 Level 3 prompts (use past perfect)
- Week 4: Complete 5 Level 4 prompts (use future perfect)
- Week 5: Practice 3 role-play scenarios
- Week 6: Complete 3 Level 5 complex scenarios

### Self-Evaluation
After each prompt, ask yourself:
- ☐ Did I speak for at least 1 minute?
- ☐ Did I use complete sentences?
- ☐ Did I use the target grammar (past/future perfect)?
- ☐ Was my pronunciation clear?
- ☐ Could a native speaker understand me?
