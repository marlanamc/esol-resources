Superlatives & Quantifiers Worksheet

Quick Reference

Quantifier Use With Example
many Countable nouns many hours, many jobs
much Uncountable nouns much time, much money
few / a few Countable (not enough / some) few options, a few friends
little / a little Uncountable (not enough / some) little hope, a little time
fewer Countable (comparison) fewer hours, fewer problems
less Uncountable (comparison) less stress, less time
Tip: Can you say "one ___"? If yes, it's countable (use many/few/fewer). If no, it's uncountable (use much/little/less).

Part B: Many or Much?

4 min

Circle the correct answer.

  1. 1. How ( many / much ) vacation days do you get per year?
  2. 2. I don't have ( many / much ) experience with computers.
  3. 3. There aren't ( many / much ) affordable apartments available.
  4. 4. This job requires too ( many / much ) travel.
  5. 5. How ( many / much ) hours do you work per week?
  6. 6. She has ( many / much ) responsibilities at work.

Part C: Few/Little and Fewer/Less

4 min

  1. 1. There are very good apartments in my budget.
  2. 2. I have very patience for rude customers.
  3. 3. This job offers vacation days than my last one.
  4. 4. I want a job with responsibility.
  5. 5. The new apartment has bedrooms than our old one.
  6. 6. Working night shift means traffic on my commute.

Part E: Real-Life Comparisons

5 min

Complete these sentences about comparing options. Use the most, the least, the fewest, or the best.

  1. 1. I looked at three apartments: A has 2 bedrooms, B has 3 bedrooms, and C has 1 bedroom. Apartment C has bedrooms.
  2. 2. I received three job offers: Job A pays $15/hour, Job B pays $18/hour, and Job C pays $20/hour. Job C pays money.
  3. 3. Of all my coworkers, Maria has experience because she's been working here for 10 years.
  4. 4. I compared three neighborhoods: Downtown has heavy traffic, Midtown has moderate traffic, and Riverside has almost no traffic. Riverside has traffic.

Part F: Positive or Negative Meaning?

4 min

Choose the correct answer based on the meaning in parentheses.

  1. 1. I have ( few / a few ) friends here, so I'm happy. (positive - I do have some friends)
  2. 2. There are ( few / a few ) good restaurants near my apartment. (negative - almost none)
  3. 3. I need ( little / a little ) help with this project. (positive - some help)
  4. 4. I have ( little / a little ) patience for rude customers. (negative - almost none)
