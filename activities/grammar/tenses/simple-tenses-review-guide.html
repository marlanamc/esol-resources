Quick Reference: The Three Simple Tenses

Present Simple

When to use: Habits, routines, facts, general truths

Subject + verb (base/s/es)
He/She/It: verb + s/es | I/You/We/They: base verb
Examples:
I work every day.
She works at a hospital.
They don't like coffee.
Do you speak English?

Time expressions: every day, always, usually, often, sometimes, never, on Mondays

Past Simple

When to use: Finished actions in the past, past habits

Subject + verb (past form)
Regular: verb + ed | Irregular: past form (went, saw, etc.)
Examples:
I worked yesterday.
She went to Paris last year.
They didn't see the movie.
Did you call him?

Time expressions: yesterday, last week/month/year, ago, in 2020, when I was young

Future Simple

When to use: Predictions, spontaneous decisions, promises, offers

Subject + will/won't + base verb
All subjects use will/won't + base verb
Examples:
I will go to the store tomorrow.
She won't call you.
Will you help me?

Time expressions: tomorrow, next week/month/year, soon, later, in 2025
