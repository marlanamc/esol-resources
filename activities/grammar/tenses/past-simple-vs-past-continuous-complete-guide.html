Welcome! Understanding Past Simple vs Past Continuous

This is one of the important comparisons in English! Many students confuse these two tenses. Understanding when to use each one will help you speak and write more accurately.

Key Difference: Past Simple = habits, routines, facts. Past Continuous = actions happening NOW or temporary situations.

Quick Comparison

Feature Past Simple Past Continuous
When to use Completed actions, finished events, specific past times Actions in progress at a specific past time, interrupted actions
Time focus Specific past time, completed At a specific past time, in progress
Form Subject + past simple verb Subject + was/were + verb-ing
Time expressions yesterday, last week, in 2019, when when, while, at that time, at 3 PM
Example I work at a school. (permanent job) I am working on a project. (happening now)

When do we use Past Simple?

Past Simple is used for completed actions, finished events, and specific past times. It describes things that are generally true or happen regularly.

Uses of Past Simple:

1. Completed Actions
I wake up at 7 AM every day.
She goes to the gym three times a week.
They have dinner together every evening.
2. Finished Events
Water boils at 100 degrees Celsius.
The sun rises in the east.
Birds fly.
3. Specific Past Times
I live in Boston.
She works as a teacher.
He speaks three languages.
4. Sequences of Events
The train leaves at 8:30 AM.
Class starts at 9:00.
5. State Verbs (Past)
I like chocolate. (NOT "I am liking")
She knows the answer. (NOT "She is knowing")
They want to go. (NOT "They are wanting")

üü£ Past Continuous

When do we use Past Continuous?

Past Continuous is used for actions happening right now, temporary situations, and future arrangements. It describes things that are happening at the moment or changing.

Uses of Past Continuous:

1. Actions in Progress at a Specific Past Time
I am reading a book right now.
She is studying for her exam.
They are playing soccer in the park.
2. Interrupted Actions
I am living in Boston this month. (temporary)
She is working at a restaurant this summer. (temporary job)
3. Parallel Actions
I am learning English this year.
She is writing a book.
4. Background Actions
I am meeting my friend tomorrow.
We are going to the movies tonight.
5. Repeated Past Actions
The weather is getting warmer.
My English is getting better.

Side-by-Side Comparisons

Completed vs In Progress:
I drink coffee every morning. (Past Simple - habit)
I am drinking coffee now. (Past Continuous - happening now)
Completed vs Interrupted:
I work at a school. (Past Simple - permanent job)
I am working on a project. (Past Continuous - temporary activity)
Sequence vs Simultaneous:
The sun rises in the east. (Past Simple - fact)
The sun is rising now. (Past Continuous - happening now)
üí° Key Point:

Ask yourself: Is this a habit/routine/fact (Past Simple) or something happening right now/temporary (Past Continuous)?
