Key Points to Remember

JUST

Use for actions that happened very recently (within the last few minutes)

Example: "She has just eaten lunch" (12:05 PM)

ALREADY

Use for completed actions, often earlier than expected

Example: "He has already woken up" (happened before now)

YET

Use for actions that haven't happened but we expect them to happen

Example: "She hasn't eaten dinner yet" (but she will)

Now It's Your Turn!

Think about YOUR day today. What have you done? What haven't you done yet?

Pair Work Discussion

  • Have you already eaten lunch today?
  • What have you just finished doing?
  • What haven't you done yet today?
  • What time did you wake up? Has your partner woken up earlier or later?

Writing Challenge

  • Write 5 sentences about your day using already, yet, or just
  • Compare your routine to Sarah, Henry, or Abigail
  • Example: "I have already eaten breakfast, but Abigail wakes up earlier than me!"

Class Activity

  • Walk around and ask 3 classmates: "What have you already done today?"
  • Find someone who has done something interesting today
  • Report back: "Abigail has already gone to the gym today!"
