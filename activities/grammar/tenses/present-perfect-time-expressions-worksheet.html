1. FOR / SINCE - Duration vs Starting Point

ðŸ“… FOR vs SINCE

FOR and SINCE both mean "from a time in the past until now", but they're used differently:

FOR = a period of time

Use FOR with a duration (how long):

I have lived here for 5 years.
She has worked here for 2 months.
We have studied English for a long time.

SINCE = a starting point

Use SINCE with a point in time (when it started):

I have lived here since 2020.
She has worked here since January.
We have studied English since last year.
ðŸ’¡ Memory Trick:

FOR = "how long" (duration) | SINCE = "when it started" (point in time)

2. ALREADY - "before now"

Already means "before now" and is used in affirmative sentences. It goes between "have/has" and the past participle.

I have already finished my homework.
She has already eaten lunch.

Practice: Complete the sentences with "already"

Write complete sentences using "have/has already + past participle".

3. YET - "until now"

Yet means "until now" and is used in negative sentences and questions. It goes at the end of the sentence.

I haven't finished my homework yet.
Have you eaten lunch yet?

Practice: Complete the sentences with "yet"

Write complete sentences using the correct form + "yet".

4. JUST - "a short time ago"

Just means "a short time ago" and is used in affirmative sentences. It goes between "have/has" and the past participle.

I have just finished my homework.
She has just arrived home.

Practice: Complete the sentences with "just"

Write complete sentences using "have/has just + past participle".

5. EVER - "at any time in your life"

Ever means "at any time in your life" and is used in questions and negative sentences. It goes between "have/has" and the past participle.

Have you ever been to Paris?
I have never been to Paris.

Practice: Complete the questions with "ever"

Write complete questions using "Have/Has + subject + ever + past participle".

6. NEVER - "not at any time in your life"

Never means "not at any time in your life" and is used in negative sentences. It goes between "have/has" and the past participle.

I have never been to Paris.
She has never eaten sushi.

Practice: Complete the sentences with "never"

Write complete sentences using "have/has never + past participle".

Questions to Ask:

  • Have you ever been to another country?
  • What have you just eaten?
  • Have you already done your laundry?
  • What have you never tried?
