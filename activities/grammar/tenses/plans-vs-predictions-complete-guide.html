Welcome! Understanding Plans vs Predictions

This is one of the important comparisons in English! Many students confuse these two tenses. Understanding when to use each one will help you speak and write more accurately.

Key Difference: Plans = habits, routines, facts. Predictions = actions happening NOW or temporary situations.

Quick Comparison

Feature Plans Predictions
When to use Intentions, arrangements, fixed plans What will happen, future events
Time focus Intended, arranged Expected, predicted
Form Going to / Present Continuous Will / Going to
Time expressions tomorrow, next week, I plan to I think, probably, maybe, I predict
Example I work at a school. (permanent job) I am working on a project. (happening now)

When do we use Plans?

Plans is used for intentions, arrangements, and fixed plans. It describes things that are generally true or happen regularly.

Uses of Plans:

1. Intentions
I wake up at 7 AM every day.
She goes to the gym three times a week.
They have dinner together every evening.
2. Arrangements
Water boils at 100 degrees Celsius.
The sun rises in the east.
Birds fly.
3. Fixed Plans
I live in Boston.
She works as a teacher.
He speaks three languages.
4. Preparations
The train leaves at 8:30 AM.
Class starts at 9:00.
5. Goals
I like chocolate. (NOT "I am liking")
She knows the answer. (NOT "She is knowing")
They want to go. (NOT "They are wanting")

üü£ Predictions

When do we use Predictions?

Predictions is used for actions happening right now, temporary situations, and future arrangements. It describes things that are happening at the moment or changing.

Uses of Predictions:

1. General Predictions
I am reading a book right now.
She is studying for her exam.
They are playing soccer in the park.
2. Evidence-Based Predictions
I am living in Boston this month. (temporary)
She is working at a restaurant this summer. (temporary job)
3. Weather Predictions
I am learning English this year.
She is writing a book.
4. Future Facts
I am meeting my friend tomorrow.
We are going to the movies tonight.
5. Opinions About Future
The weather is getting warmer.
My English is getting better.

Side-by-Side Comparisons

Plan vs Prediction:
I drink coffee every morning. (Plans - habit)
I am drinking coffee now. (Predictions - happening now)
Intention vs Expectation:
I work at a school. (Plans - permanent job)
I am working on a project. (Predictions - temporary activity)
Arrangement vs Prediction:
The sun rises in the east. (Plans - fact)
The sun is rising now. (Predictions - happening now)
üí° Key Point:

Ask yourself: Is this a habit/routine/fact (Plans) or something happening right now/temporary (Predictions)?
