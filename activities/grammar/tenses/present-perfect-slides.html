Present Perfect

Connecting the Past to the Present

Intermediate Grammar

Learning Objectives

  • Understand when to use Present Perfect
  • Learn how to form positive, negative, and question sentences
  • Master past participles (regular and irregular)
  • Use time expressions: for, since, already, yet, just
  • Know the difference between Past Simple and Present Perfect

What is Present Perfect?

Present Perfect connects the past to the present.

I have been to Paris.

This is a life experience - no specific time mentioned

We use it to talk about:

  • Life experiences
  • Unfinished actions
  • Recent actions
  • Past actions with present results

When Do We Use Present Perfect?

1. Life Experiences

I have been to Paris three times.

2. Unfinished Actions

I have lived here for 5 years. (still living)

3. Recent Actions

I have just finished my homework.

4. Present Results

I have lost my keys. (I don't have them now)

Past Participles - Regular Verbs

For regular verbs, add -ed to the base form:

Base Form
work
play
study
live
→
Past Participle
worked
played
studied
lived
Spelling rules: -e → add -d | consonant + y → change y to i + -ed

Past Participles - Irregular Verbs

Irregular verbs have different forms - you must memorize them!

Base Form
be
go
see
do
eat
write
→
Past Participle
been
gone
seen
done
eaten
written
Teacher note: Practice common irregular verbs regularly!

Past Simple vs Present Perfect

Past Simple
✅ Specific time mentioned

I went to Paris in 2019.

She studied English last year.

Present Perfect
✅ Connection to present

I have been to Paris. (no specific time)

She has studied English for 2 years. (still studying)

Quick test: Is there a specific past time? YES → Past Simple | NO → Present Perfect

Time Expressions: FOR & SINCE

FOR

Duration (how long)

  • for 5 years
  • for 2 months
  • for a week

I have lived here for 5 years.

SINCE

Starting point (when it started)

  • since 2020
  • since January
  • since Monday

I have lived here since 2020.

Memory trick: FOR = "how long" | SINCE = "when it started"

Time Expressions: ALREADY, YET, JUST

✅ ALREADY

"before now" - in affirmative sentences

I have already finished my homework.

❌ YET

"until now" - in negative sentences & questions

I haven't finished my homework yet.

⚡ JUST

"a short time ago" - in affirmative sentences

I have just finished my homework.

Common Mistakes

❌ Wrong: I have went to Paris.
✅ Correct: I have gone to Paris.

Use past participle, not past simple!

❌ Wrong: She have been to Paris.
✅ Correct: She has been to Paris.

Use "has" with he/she/it!

❌ Wrong: I have been to Paris in 2019.
✅ Correct: I went to Paris in 2019.

Specific time → Use Past Simple!

Practice

Complete the sentences with the correct form:

  1. I _____ (be) to London three times.
  2. She _____ (not finish) her homework yet.
  3. _____ you _____ (see) that movie?
  4. They _____ (live) here for 5 years.
  5. He _____ (just arrive) at the station.
Answers:
1. have been | 2. hasn't finished | 3. Have...seen | 4. have lived | 5. has just arrived

Summary

Present Perfect = have/has + past participle
Use for: life experiences, unfinished actions, recent actions
FOR = duration | SINCE = starting point
ALREADY = affirmative | YET = negative/questions
No specific time → Present Perfect | Specific time → Past Simple
