Reported Speech - Step-by-Step Guide

Complete Guide Intermediate

Welcome! Let's Learn Reported Speech Together

We're going to learn Reported Speech (also called Indirect Speech) step by step. Don't worry - we'll practice each part before moving to the next. Take your time and work through each section!

What is Reported Speech? It's when you tell someone what another person said, without using their exact words. Instead of quoting them directly, you report what they said.

What is Reported Speech? Understanding the Basics

Direct Speech vs Reported Speech

When we use Direct Speech, we repeat someone's exact words using quotation marks. When we use Reported Speech, we tell someone what another person said, but we change the words and structure.

üìå Direct Speech (Exact Words)

Tom said, "I am tired."
Maria said, "I will help you."
John asked, "Where are you going?"

üí° Direct speech uses quotation marks and the exact words.

üìå Reported Speech (What They Said)

Tom said (that) he was tired.
Maria said (that) she would help me.
John asked where I was going.

üí° Reported speech changes pronouns, tenses, and word order. No quotation marks!

üí° Key Point:

Reported Speech is used when you want to tell someone what another person said, asked, or told you. It's very common in conversations, stories, and news reports!

1

Tense Changes in Reported Speech

Why do tenses change?

When we report what someone said, we usually move the tense back in time (one step to the past). This is because the original statement was said in the past, so we report it from a past perspective.

Direct Speech Reported Speech
Present Simple
"I work here."		 Past Simple
He said (that) he worked there.
Present Continuous
"I am working now."		 Past Continuous
He said (that) he was working then.
Present Perfect
"I have finished."		 Past Perfect
He said (that) he had finished.
Past Simple
"I went to Paris."		 Past Perfect
He said (that) he had gone to Paris.
Will
"I will help you."		 Would
He said (that) he would help me.
Can
"I can speak English."		 Could
He said (that) he could speak English.
Must
"I must go now."		 Had to
He said (that) he had to go then.
üí° Important Exceptions:
  • If the reporting verb is in the present tense (says, tells), we don't change the tense!
  • Example: "She says she is tired." (present reporting verb = no tense change)
  • If the information is still true or always true, we sometimes don't change the tense.
  • Example: "He said the Earth revolves around the sun." (fact = can stay present)

2

Say, Tell, and Ask - Which One Do I Use?

The Three Main Reporting Verbs

These are the most common verbs used in reported speech. Each one has specific rules about how to use it!

üìå SAY

Say is used for general statements. It doesn't need an object (person) after it.

Direct: "I am happy."
Reported: She said (that) she was happy.
Direct: "I will come tomorrow."
Reported: He said (that) he would come the next day.

üí° Structure: say + (that) + clause

üìå TELL

Tell is used when you mention WHO you told. It always needs an object (person) after it.

Direct: "I am happy."
Reported: She told me (that) she was happy.
Direct: "I will help you."
Reported: He told her (that) he would help her.

üí° Structure: tell + person + (that) + clause

üìå ASK

Ask is used for questions. It can be used with or without a person.

Direct: "Where are you going?"
Reported: She asked where I was going.
Direct: "Can you help me?"
Reported: He asked me if I could help him.

üí° Structure: ask (+ person) + question word/if + clause

üí° Key Point:

Say = general statements (no person needed)

Tell = statements with a person (tell me, tell him, tell her, etc.)

Ask = questions

Remember: You can't say "He said me" ‚ùå - Use "He told me" ‚úÖ or "He said to me" ‚úÖ

Reported Statements - Step by Step

How to Report Statements

When reporting statements (not questions or commands), follow these steps:

üìå Step-by-Step Process:

  1. Remove quotation marks
  2. Change pronouns (I ‚Üí he/she, you ‚Üí I/me, etc.)
  3. Change tenses (move one step back in time)
  4. Change time/place words (now ‚Üí then, today ‚Üí that day, etc.)
  5. Add reporting verb (said, told, etc.)

üìå Examples:

Direct: "I am studying English."
Reported: She said (that) she was studying English.
Direct: "I will help you tomorrow."
Reported: He told me (that) he would help me the next day.
Direct: "I have finished my work."
Reported: She said (that) she had finished her work.

4

Reported Questions - Yes/No and Wh- Questions

How to Report Questions

Reporting questions is different from reporting statements. The word order changes back to statement order (subject + verb), and we use if or whether for yes/no questions.

üìå Yes/No Questions

For yes/no questions, use if or whether and change the word order to statement order.

Direct: "Are you tired?"
Reported: He asked if I was tired.
Direct: "Can you help me?"
Reported: She asked whether I could help her.
Direct: "Do you like pizza?"
Reported: He asked if I liked pizza.

üí° Notice: No question mark, and word order is statement order (subject + verb).

üìå Wh- Questions (What, Where, When, Who, Why, How)

For wh- questions, keep the question word and change the word order to statement order.

Direct: "Where are you going?"
Reported: She asked where I was going.
Direct: "What time is it?"
Reported: He asked what time it was.
Direct: "Why did you leave?"
Reported: She asked why I had left.

üí° Keep the question word, but change word order to statement order and change tenses.

üí° Key Point:

In reported questions, the word order changes from question order to statement order!

  • Question: "Where are you going?" (question order)
  • Reported: "She asked where I was going." (statement order)

5

Reported Commands and Requests

How to Report Commands and Requests

When reporting commands (orders) or requests, we use tell/ask + person + (not) + to + infinitive.

üìå Commands (Orders)

Use tell + person + to + infinitive

Direct: "Close the door!"
Reported: He told me to close the door.
Direct: "Don't be late!"
Reported: She told him not to be late.
Direct: "Stop talking!"
Reported: The teacher told the students to stop talking.

üìå Requests (Polite Commands)

Use ask + person + to + infinitive

Direct: "Please help me."
Reported: She asked me to help her.
Direct: "Could you open the window?"
Reported: He asked me to open the window.
Direct: "Don't forget to call me."
Reported: She asked me not to forget to call her.
üí° Key Point:

Tell = commands/orders (stronger)

Ask = requests (polite)

Structure: tell/ask + person + (not) + to + infinitive

6

Time and Place Changes

Why Do Time and Place Words Change?

When we report speech, time and place references need to change because the situation has changed. What was "today" when they spoke might be "yesterday" when we report it!

Direct Speech Reported Speech
now then / at that moment
today that day
tomorrow the next day / the following day
yesterday the day before / the previous day
next week/month/year the following week/month/year
last week/month/year the week/month/year before
here there
this that
these those
ago before / earlier

üìå Examples:

Direct: "I will call you tomorrow."
Reported: She said (that) she would call me the next day.
Direct: "I saw him yesterday."
Reported: He said (that) he had seen him the day before.
Direct: "I am here now."
Reported: She said (that) she was there then.
üí° Important:

If you're reporting something that was said on the same day, you might not need to change "today" ‚Üí "today" is still correct!

Example: "She said (that) she would call me today." (if reporting on the same day)

7

Other Reporting Verbs

More Ways to Report Speech

Besides say, tell, and ask, there are many other reporting verbs that can make your English more interesting and precise!

üìå Common Reporting Verbs:

admit - to confess
"I made a mistake." ‚Üí He admitted (that) he had made a mistake.
agree - to say yes
"Yes, I'll help." ‚Üí She agreed to help.
deny - to say no/not true
"I didn't do it." ‚Üí He denied doing it.
promise - to commit
"I'll be there." ‚Üí She promised (that) she would be there.
suggest - to propose
"Let's go to the park." ‚Üí He suggested going to the park.
warn - to caution
"Be careful!" ‚Üí She warned me to be careful.
explain - to clarify
"It works like this..." ‚Üí He explained how it worked.
insist - to demand
"You must come!" ‚Üí She insisted (that) I come.
üí° Key Point:

Different reporting verbs can be followed by different structures:

  • verb + (that) + clause: say, tell, admit, explain, promise
  • verb + to + infinitive: agree, promise, refuse
  • verb + person + to + infinitive: ask, tell, warn, advise
  • verb + gerund: suggest, deny, admit
