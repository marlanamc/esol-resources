Speaking Activities: Simple vs Continuous Tenses

Use these activities to practice speaking after completing the worksheet

Activity 1: Find Someone Who...

üìã Instructions:
  1. Walk around the classroom and ask different classmates questions
  2. When someone answers "Yes," write their name and ask a follow-up question
  3. Try to find a different person for each question
  4. Use Present Simple for habits and Present Continuous for current actions/plans
Find someone who...
... usually wakes up before 7 AM
Follow-up: "What time do you usually wake up?"
Find someone who...
... is currently learning something new
Follow-up: "What are you learning?"
Find someone who...
... often exercises in the morning
Follow-up: "What kind of exercise do you do?"
Find someone who...
... is planning a trip next month
Follow-up: "Where are you going?"
Find someone who...
... never drinks coffee
Follow-up: "What do you drink instead?"
Find someone who...
... is this week working on a special project
Follow-up: "What project are you working on?"
Find someone who...
... sometimes reads before bed
Follow-up: "What kind of books do you read?"
Find someone who...
... is meeting someone this weekend
Follow-up: "Who are you meeting?"
üí° Teacher Tip: After students finish, ask them to share one interesting thing they learned about a classmate.

Activity 2: What Were You Doing When...?

üìã Instructions:
  1. Work in pairs or small groups
  2. Take turns asking "What were you doing when [event]?"
  3. Use Past Continuous for the ongoing action and Past Simple for the interruption
  4. Be creative with your answers!
Example Conversation:
A: "What were you doing when the power went out yesterday?"
B: "I was cooking dinner when the power went out. I had to finish cooking by candlelight!"
What were you doing when...
  • ... your phone rang this morning?
  • ... it started raining last week?
  • ... your teacher called your name in class?
  • ... the fire alarm went off?
  • ... you received an important email?
  • ... your friend texted you last night?
  • ... you heard loud music outside?
  • ... your favorite TV show started?
üí° Extension: After sharing, choose one story and have the student tell it to the whole class.

Activity 3: Making Plans - Role Play

üìã Instructions:
  1. Work in pairs
  2. One person is the "Organizer," the other is the "Busy Friend"
  3. Try to make plans together using Present Continuous for scheduled plans and Future Continuous for what will be happening
  4. Switch roles and try again

Role A: The Organizer

Your goal: Plan a fun activity with your friend this weekend

Suggestions:

  • Going to a movie
  • Having dinner together
  • Going to a concert
  • Playing sports
  • Visiting a museum

Useful phrases:

  • "Are you doing anything on Saturday?"
  • "Would you like to...?"
  • "I'm going to... this weekend"

Role B: The Busy Friend

Your goal: You have a busy schedule, but you want to find time to meet

Your schedule:

  • Saturday morning: working
  • Saturday afternoon: studying
  • Sunday morning: free
  • Sunday evening: visiting family

Useful phrases:

  • "I'm working/studying at that time"
  • "I'll be free at..."
  • "At 3 PM, I'll be studying"
Example Conversation:
A: "Are you doing anything on Saturday afternoon? I'm going to a concert and I'd love you to come!"
B: "I'm studying at 2 PM on Saturday, but I'll be free after 4 PM. What time is the concert?"
A: "It starts at 7 PM. Are you free then?"
B: "Yes! At 7 PM, I'll be finishing my studies, so that works perfectly. I'm coming with you!"

Activity 4: A Day in the Life (Present Habits vs Plans)

üìã Instructions:
  1. Work in pairs or small groups
  2. Describe your usual day using Present Simple
  3. Then describe what you're doing today or this week using Present Continuous
  4. Compare: What's different? What's the same?
Example:
My usual day: "I usually wake up at 7 AM. I always have coffee for breakfast. I normally work from 9 AM to 5 PM. I often go to the gym after work."

This week: "I'm waking up at 6 AM this week because I'm starting a new project. I'm working longer hours today. I'm not going to the gym this week because I'm too busy."

Guide Questions:

Morning Routine:
  • What time do you usually wake up?
  • What are you doing today that's different?
Work/School:
  • What do you normally do during the day?
  • What are you working on this week?
Evening:
  • What do you often do in the evening?
  • What are you doing tonight?

Activity 5: Future Time Capsule

üìã Instructions:
  1. Imagine it's one year from now or five years from now
  2. Describe what you will be doing at that time
  3. Use Future Continuous for ongoing actions
  4. Share with a partner or small group
Example:
"This time next year, I will be studying at university. I'll be living in a dormitory. At 10 AM on a typical day, I'll be attending classes. I'll be making new friends and learning new things every day."

Time Capsule Prompts:

One Year From Now:
  • What will you be doing this time next year?
  • Where will you be living?
  • What will you be studying or working on?
  • What will you be doing at this time next year?
Five Years From Now:
  • What career will you be pursuing?
  • What will you be doing on a typical Monday?
  • What skills will you be using?
üí° Extension: Have students write their "time capsule" predictions and share the most interesting ones with the class.

Activity 6: Interruption Storytelling

üìã Instructions:
  1. Work in groups of 3-4
  2. One person starts telling a story using Past Continuous
  3. Another person interrupts with a question or event using Past Simple
  4. Continue the story, taking turns with interruptions
  5. Create a fun, creative story together!
Example Story:
Person 1: "I was walking through the park yesterday..."
Person 2: "...when suddenly a dog started barking at me!"
Person 1: "Yes! I was running away when I tripped over a tree root."
Person 3: "What happened next?"
Person 1: "I was getting up when someone helped me..."
Person 4: "...and that person was my old friend from high school!"

Story Starters:

  • I was cooking dinner when...
  • I was studying for my exam when...
  • I was walking home when...
  • I was waiting for the bus when...
  • I was talking on the phone when...
  • I was shopping at the mall when...

üìù Teacher Notes

Suggested Sequence:

  1. Warm-up: Activity 1 (Find Someone Who) - Gets students moving and talking
  2. Practice: Activity 2 (What Were You Doing When...?) - Reinforces Past Continuous + Past Simple
  3. Application: Activity 3 (Making Plans) - Real-world use of Present/Future Continuous
  4. Comparison: Activity 4 (A Day in the Life) - Contrasts Simple vs Continuous
  5. Creative: Activities 5 & 6 - More fun, less structured practice

Tips for Success:

  • Model the activity first with a student or give clear examples
  • Circulate and listen for correct tense usage - give gentle corrections
  • Encourage students to use time words from the worksheet
  • Have students switch partners/groups between activities
  • Do a quick review of key differences before starting
  • End with a group discussion about what was difficult or easy

Differentiation:

  • Lower level: Provide sentence starters, focus on one tense at a time
  • Higher level: Add more complex scenarios, require longer responses
  • Mixed levels: Pair stronger students with those who need support
