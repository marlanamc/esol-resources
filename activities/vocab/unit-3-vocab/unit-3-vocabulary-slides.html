Intermediate Vocabulary

Not required to pay - When a fee or cost is waived, you don't have to pay it.

Example Sentence

Tab

Sounds like: "tab" (rhymes with "cab")

Definition

A section on a website you can click - Tabs help organize information on websites and apps, like tabs in a filing cabinet.

Example Sentence

"Click on the 'Contact Us' tab at the top of the website to find our phone number."

ðŸ’¡ Tip: You'll see tabs on almost every website - they're like labels that help you find different pages or sections quickly!

Errands

Sounds like: "AIR-uhndz"

Definition

Small tasks outside the home - Things you need to do away from home, like shopping, mailing letters, or picking up dry cleaning.

Example Sentence

"I need to run some errands this afternoon - I have to buy groceries, pick up my prescription, and drop off a package at the post office."

ðŸ’¡ Tip: We often say "run errands" - it means to go out and complete several small tasks in one trip. Very common in daily conversation!

Navigate

Sounds like: "NAV-ih-gate"

Definition

To find your way or move through a place or website - To successfully move around, whether in a physical space or on a computer/website.

Example Sentence

"It's easy to navigate the new website because the menu is clearly organized."

ðŸ’¡ Tip: You can navigate a city, a building, a website, or even a difficult situation. It's all about finding your way successfully!

Urgently

Sounds like: "UR-juhnt-lee"

Definition

Very quickly or needing fast help - When something is urgent, it needs immediate attention and cannot wait.

Example Sentence

"The patient needs to see a doctor urgently - his condition is getting worse quickly."

ðŸ’¡ Tip: This word is often used in medical, business, or emergency situations. It's stronger than "soon" - it means RIGHT NOW!

Definition

An event to collect money for a cause - A special event or activity organized to raise money for a charity, school, organization, or important project.

ðŸ’¡ Tip: Common fundraisers include bake sales, car washes, charity runs, auctions, and benefit concerts. They're a great way to support causes you care about!

Definition

A building where legal matters happen - The place where judges, lawyers, and juries work to resolve legal cases and disputes.

Example Sentence

ðŸ’¡ Tip: You might visit a courthouse for jury duty, to get married, to file legal documents, or to attend a trial. It's an important government building!

Definition

A beginner-level job - A position that requires little or no previous work experience, perfect for people just starting their career.

ðŸ’¡ Tip: Entry-level jobs are great for building experience and learning new skills. They often lead to better opportunities as you gain experience!

Definition

Not too expensive; low cost - Something that you can buy without spending too much money, usually within your budget.

Example Sentence

ðŸ’¡ Tip: This word is very common in advertisements and when talking about housing, food, education, or healthcare. It means you can afford it!

Definition

To move to a new place - To leave your current location and establish your home or business in a different city, state, or country.

ðŸ’¡ Tip: This is a formal word often used in business contexts. People relocate for jobs, family, or better opportunities. It's more formal than "move."

Definition

Money or things given to help others - Something you give freely to support a cause, charity, or person in need, without expecting anything in return.

ðŸ’¡ Tip: You can make donations of money, food, clothes, blood, or time. Many donations are tax-deductible, which means you can reduce your taxes!
