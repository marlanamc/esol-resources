Section 1: Connectors (while, because, but, so, when)

Quick Review

Connectors help us join ideas and show relationships between sentences.

  • while = at the same time as / during the time that
  • because = reason / cause (answers "why?")
  • but = contrast / opposite idea
  • so = result / consequence (answers "what happened because of this?")
  • when = at the time that / at the moment that
while: I was studying while my sister was watching TV.
because: I stayed home because it was raining.
but: I wanted to go out, but I was too tired.
so: It was raining, so I stayed home.
when: I was reading a book when the phone rang.

Section 3: Present Continuous vs Future Continuous

Quick Review

Feature Present Continuous Future Continuous
When Actions happening NOW or future arrangements Actions in progress at a specific future time
Form am/is/are + verb-ing will be + verb-ing
Time words now, at the moment, tonight, tomorrow at 3pm tomorrow, this time next week, in 2 hours

Section 4: Past Continuous with Past Simple

Quick Review

We often use Past Continuous and Past Simple together in one sentence:

  • Past Continuous = the longer action that was in progress
  • Past Simple = the shorter action that interrupted it
I was walking to school when I saw my friend.
While I was studying, my phone rang.
She was cooking dinner when the doorbell rang.
ðŸ’¡ Remember: Use when or while to connect the two actions. Usually: "was/were + verb-ing" (longer action) + "when" + "past simple" (shorter action).

Section 5: Determiners

Quick Review

Determiners come before nouns and tell us which thing or how many things we're talking about.

Type Determiners Example
Articles a, an, the I saw a cat. The cat was black.
Demonstratives this, that, these, those This book is mine. Those students are new.
Possessives my, your, his, her, its, our, their My car is red. Their house is big.
Quantifiers some, any, many, much, few, little, all, both I have some money. There aren't many people here.
