Reading

Perfect tenses show RESULT: what you have completed. "I have taken the medicine" means you finished taking it - it shows the result. Perfect Continuous tenses show DURATION: how long something has been happening. "I have been taking the medicine for a week" means you started a week ago and you're still taking it - it shows duration.

When talking to doctors, use Present Perfect for completed actions: "I have taken all my pills." Use Present Perfect Continuous for ongoing actions: "I have been feeling better since yesterday." Past Perfect shows what happened before another past event: "I had recovered before my follow-up appointment." Future Perfect shows what will be completed by a future time: "I will have finished the treatment by next month."

1) What is the difference between "I have taken the medicine" and "I have been taking the medicine"?
2) When would you use Past Perfect versus Past Perfect Continuous?
3) How can Future Perfect help you talk about your health recovery timeline?

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Imagine you went to the doctor last week because you had chest pain for 5 days. The doctor told you to rest and take medicine. Write about your visit using Perfect tenses. What have you done since the visit? (Present Perfect - result) What have you been doing? (Present Perfect Continuous - duration) What had you done before the visit? (Past Perfect) Use both result and duration tenses.

Optional challenge: Use Future Perfect to talk about when you will have finished your treatment.
