Reading

Stress is a normal part of life, but too much stress can harm your health. Having a plan helps you cope better. Type 1 conditionals are useful for creating stress management plans: "If I start to feel stressed, I will notice my body's signals. If I'm breathing fast, I will do breathing exercises."

Work stress is common. If your job gets too busy, you could try prioritizing tasks. If your boss asks you to work overtime, you could explain your schedule. Setting boundaries is an important self-care skill. If you feel overwhelmed, you should ask for help rather than trying to do everything alone.

Remember that taking care of yourself is not selfish. If you can't sleep, you could try meditation or herbal tea. If you feel anxious, you could call a friend or go for a walk. Having healthy coping strategies will help you manage stress better.

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Create your personal stress management plan using Type 1 conditionals. Explain what you will do if you feel stressed, overwhelmed, or can't sleep.
  • Try using multiple "If... I will..." statements for different stress situations
  • Consider including specific strategies that work for you
  • Try explaining what you will do for work stress versus personal stress
  • Could describe when you will ask for help or set boundaries

Optional challenge: Include a chain of actions (If A doesn't work, I will try B, and if that doesn't work, I will...).
