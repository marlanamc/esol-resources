Reading

When you pick up medicine at the pharmacy, you receive important instructions. These instructions are written on the bottle label. Passive voice is commonly used because it focuses on what should be done, not who does it.

For example, "This medicine is taken three times a day" is clearer than "You should take this medicine three times a day" for a label. The prescription is refilled by calling the pharmacy or your doctor. It should be stored properly—some medicines must be kept in the refrigerator, while others are stored at room temperature. You are advised to read all warnings carefully. Side effects may be experienced, such as drowsiness or nausea.

Understanding pharmacy instructions keeps you safe and helps your medicine work properly. Always ask questions if something is unclear.

1) Why is passive voice useful for pharmacy instructions?
2) What are two examples of side effects that may be experienced?
3) What should you do if pharmacy instructions are unclear?

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Write pharmacy instructions for a common medicine (like pain reliever or cold medicine) using passive voice.
  • Try explaining how the medicine is taken (dosage and timing)
  • Consider describing how it should be stored
  • Try including warnings or advice ("You are advised to...")
  • Could mention possible side effects using "may be experienced"

Optional challenge: Include when the prescription is refilled and what to do if symptoms continue.
