Reading

When you visit a clinic, certain procedures are followed. First, you are asked to check in at the front desk. Your insurance card is checked by the receptionist. Then, forms are filled out with your medical history and current symptoms.

Next, your name is called, and you are taken to an exam room. Your temperature and blood pressure are taken by a nurse. The nurse asks questions about why you came in. After that, the doctor examines you. If needed, tests are ordered. Finally, you are given instructions for medicine or follow-up care.

Understanding these steps helps you feel more comfortable. Passive voice is useful for describing procedures because it focuses on what happens, not who does it.

1) What is the first thing you are asked to do when you arrive at a clinic?
2) Who takes your temperature and blood pressure?
3) Why is passive voice useful when explaining clinic procedures?

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Describe what happens during a clinic visit using passive voice. Put the steps in order from check-in to leaving.
  • Try using sequencing words (First, Next, Then, Finally)
  • Consider describing at least 4-5 steps of the visit
  • Try using passive voice phrases like "are asked," "is taken," "are given"
  • Could explain why each step is important

Optional challenge: Include "by" phrases to say who does each action (e.g., "Your temperature is taken by a nurse").
