Reading

When you feel sick, it's important to know what advice to follow. Different modals show different levels of importance. "Should" gives general advice, like "You should drink water." "Must" and "have to" show something is necessary: "You must take this medicine every day." "Could" offers suggestions: "You could try resting at home first."

If you have a fever over 103°F, you must see a doctor right away. This is serious. For a mild headache, you could try drinking water or taking over-the-counter medicine. If symptoms don't improve after a few days, you should call your doctor. You need to know when to get help.

Remember, you shouldn't ignore serious symptoms. If you're contagious, you have to stay home from work. Taking care of yourself helps you recover faster.
