Reading

Daily self-care is essential for holistic health. Imperatives give you clear instructions: "Take breaks. Practice breathing. Set boundaries." These commands tell you what actions to take. Declaratives explain the benefits: "Breaks restore your energy. Breathing reduces stress. Boundaries protect your well-being."

Self-care routines help you maintain balance. Start with simple imperatives: "Be mindful of your stress. Rest when you need to. Recharge regularly." Then understand why with declaratives: "Mindfulness helps you notice problems early. Rest restores your body. Recharging prevents burnout."

Remember: Imperatives are commands (Do this). Declaratives are facts (This is true). Use both to create effective self-care routines.

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Write about your daily self-care routine using imperatives and declaratives. Include 3-4 imperatives (commands) about what you do for self-care, and 3-4 declaratives (statements) explaining why these practices are important for your well-being.

Optional challenge: Use negative imperatives (Don't...) to explain what you avoid doing for self-care.
