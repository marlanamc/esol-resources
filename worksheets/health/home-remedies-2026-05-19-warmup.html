Reading

Home remedies are simple treatments you can try at home for mild symptoms. Imperatives give instructions: "Rest when you have a cold. Drink fluids. Gargle with salt water." These commands tell you what to do. Declaratives explain why: "Rest helps your body heal. Fluids relieve dehydration. Salt water can soothe a sore throat."

Common home remedies include rest, fluids, honey for a cough, ginger for nausea, and a cold compress for a headache. Use imperatives to give advice: "Apply ice to a bruise. Sip warm tea." Use declaratives to explain: "Ice relieves swelling. Warm liquids soothe the throat."

Remember: Imperatives are commands (Do this). Declaratives are facts (This is true). Use both when talking about home remedies. For serious symptoms, always see a doctor.
