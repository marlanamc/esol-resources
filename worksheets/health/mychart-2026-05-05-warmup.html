Reading

When you communicate with your doctor's office, you often need to relay the information to family or friends. Reported speech helps you explain what the doctor or receptionist said. Instead of quoting directly, you report the main message.

For example, if the doctor says, "Your test results are normal," you might tell your family, "The doctor said that my test results are normal." If the receptionist asks, "Do you have insurance?" you could relay this as "They asked if I have insurance." The message might say, "Please schedule a follow-up," which becomes "The message said that I should schedule a follow-up."

Reported speech changes pronouns and sometimes verb tenses. This skill is useful when explaining medical information, making it easier for others to understand what happened at your appointment.

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Describe a recent or imagined doctor visit or MyChart message. Tell what the doctor, nurse, or message said using reported speech.
  • Try using "said that" or "told me that" to report statements
  • Consider using "asked if" or "asked me to" to report questions or requests
  • Try using "wanted to know" for information requests
  • Could explain why the information was important to you

Optional challenge: Report a complete conversation with multiple exchanges (what they said, what you asked, what they answered).
