Reading

Understanding food labels is an important skill for healthy eating. Many people used to ignore the nutrition information on packages. They would just buy whatever looked good. Now, more people are used to checking calories, sugar, and other ingredients before making choices.

When you first start reading labels, it feels overwhelming. There's so much information! However, after a few weeks, you start getting used to it. You learn which numbers matter most for your health. What used to be confusing becomes clearer.

Some changes take time. If you're used to eating processed foods, fresh foods might taste bland at first. You might still be getting used to lower sugar or salt. That's normal—your taste buds need time to adjust.

1) What did many people use to do with food labels?
2) What happens after a few weeks of reading food labels?
3) Why might fresh foods taste bland to someone who is used to eating processed foods?

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Write about how your eating habits have changed. What did you use to eat? What are you used to eating now? What are you still getting used to?
  • Try using "used to" to describe past eating habits
  • Consider using "I'm used to" to describe current comfortable habits
  • Try using "I'm getting used to" for habits you're still adjusting to
  • Could explain why you made these changes or what helped you

Optional challenge: Compare two different time periods (e.g., "Five years ago I used to..., but now I'm used to...").
