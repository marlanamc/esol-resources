Reading

Food labels are required by law on all packaged foods. This information is listed on the package to help consumers make healthy choices. Ingredients are listed in order from most to least, so you can see what is used most in the product.

Nutrition facts are measured per serving size. Calories, sugar, and sodium are listed clearly. This information is updated regularly to reflect current health guidelines. Food labels are being improved to make them easier to understand.

Passive voice is useful when describing food processing because it focuses on what happens to the food, not who makes it. For example, "Food is processed in factories" focuses on the food, not the workers. "Ingredients are preserved" tells us what happens to the ingredients.
