Level up: Remember: Present Perfect Continuous = have/has + been + verb + -ing. Use it to talk about actions that started in the past and are still happening now.

Reading

When you visit a doctor, it's important to describe your symptoms clearly. Present Perfect Continuous helps you explain how long you have been experiencing symptoms: "I have been coughing for three days" is more specific than "I am coughing."

Doctors need to know when symptoms started and how long they have been happening. "I have been having headaches since Monday" tells the doctor the duration. This helps them understand your condition better and provide the right care.

1) Why is it important to describe symptoms clearly to a doctor?
2) How does Present Perfect Continuous help you describe symptoms?
3) What information does "I have been having headaches since Monday" give to a doctor?

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Imagine you have just come down with a fever and cough. The fever started on Monday morning, and the cough started three days ago. You have been staying home and resting. Write about your symptoms using Present Perfect Continuous. How long have you been feeling sick? When did it start? What have you been doing? Use time expressions like "for" and "since." Example: "I have been coughing for three days. I have been having a fever since Monday morning."

Optional challenge: Use "I have been waiting for..." to describe waiting for appointments or prescriptions.
