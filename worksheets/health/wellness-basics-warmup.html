Reading

Making wellness changes is not easy. Many people used to have unhealthy habits like skipping meals or staying up late. When you first start a new routine, it feels strange. You might not be used to exercising every morning or drinking eight glasses of water each day.

The good news is that your body and mind can adjust. After a few weeks, you start getting used to the new habits. What used to be difficult becomes easier. For example, if you're used to eating breakfast now, you probably feel hungry in the morning. Your body is accustomed to the new routine.

However, some changes take longer. You might still be getting used to meditating or going to bed early. That's normal. Keep practicing, and eventually these wellness habits will feel natural.

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Write about your wellness journey. What habits did you use to have? What are you used to now? What are you still getting used to?
  • Try including one past habit using "used to"
  • Consider describing what you're accustomed to now using "I'm used to"
  • Try explaining what you're still adjusting to with "I'm getting used to"
  • Could add specific examples or reasons to support your ideas

Optional challenge: Use "even though" to show contrast (e.g., "Even though it's hard, I'm getting used to...").
