Level up: Remember: "used to" = past habit, "be used to" = accustomed to, "would rather" = preference.

Reading

Making health changes requires adjustment. Many people used to have unhealthy habits. When you start a new routine, you might not be used to it yet. "I used to skip breakfast" shows a past habit. "I'm getting used to eating breakfast" shows you're adjusting to a new habit.

"Would rather" helps you express preferences during change. "I would rather exercise in the morning than at night" shows what you prefer. This helps you make choices that fit your lifestyle. After time, you become used to your new routine, and the transition feels easier.

1) What is the difference between "I used to skip breakfast" and "I'm getting used to eating breakfast"?
2) How can "would rather" help you during health changes?
3) What is one health change you would rather make than keep your old habit?

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Imagine you moved to the US six months ago and your eating habits have changed. You used to eat home-cooked meals every day, but now you are getting used to eating out more often. You would rather cook at home, but you don't have much time. Write about these health changes and adjustments. What did you use to do? What are you getting used to? What would you rather do now? Use "used to," "be used to," and "would rather" in your writing.

Optional challenge: Use "I'd rather not" to express what you prefer to avoid (e.g., "I'd rather not skip meals anymore").
