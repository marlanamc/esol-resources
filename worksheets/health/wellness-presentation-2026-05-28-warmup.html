Reading

Giving a wellness presentation is a chance to share what works for you. When you talk about wellness, certain verbs help you sound professional and natural. Use "I enjoy..." to share personal experiences. Use "I recommend..." or "I suggest..." to give advice. Use "I avoid..." or "I quit..." to explain what you changed.

A good presentation has a clear structure. Start by saying what you enjoy doing: "I enjoy walking for 20 minutes every morning." Then recommend something to the audience: "I recommend trying it for at least one week." Share what you avoid: "I avoid checking my phone before bed." Finally, give suggestions: "Consider starting with just one small change."

Practice using these verbs naturally. Instead of saying "You should do meditation," try "I suggest trying meditation" or "Consider starting with 5 minutes." These phrases sound more friendly and less bossy. Your audience will listen better when you share your experience rather than giving orders.

  • Consider using "suggest" or "consider" to give advice to your audience
  • Try using "avoid" or "quit" to explain what you changed
  • Could organize your ideas clearly: what you do, what you recommend, why it helps

Optional challenge: Include transitional phrases like "First, I...", "Also, I...", "Finally, I suggest..." to organize your presentation.
