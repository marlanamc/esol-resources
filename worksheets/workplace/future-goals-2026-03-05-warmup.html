Part B: Phrases

8 min

Future Perfect = will have + past participle (completed by a future time). Future Perfect Continuous = will have been + verb-ing (how long by that future time). Read the examples, then write your own. Try to use vocabulary from Part A.

1) By December, I will have… (Future Perfect)
  • By December, I will have saved $2,400 for my emergency fund.
  • By December, I will have achieved my first milestone.
Your turn:
2) By next year, I will have been ___ (save/invest/work) … for ___ (Future Perfect Continuous)
  • By next year, I will have been saving for a house for three years.
  • By next year, I will have been working at my job for five years.
Your turn:
3) In five years, I will have… (Future Perfect)
  • In five years, I will have paid off my debt.
  • In five years, I will have built a good emergency fund.
Your turn:
4) By the time I [reach a goal], I will have been ___ (work/save) … for ___ (Future Perfect Continuous)
  • By the time I buy a car, I will have been saving for two years.
  • By the time I retire, I will have been working for 30 years.
Your turn:
5) Will you have… by then? (Future Perfect)
  • Will you have saved enough for your priority by then?
  • Will you have invested any money by then?
Your turn:

Level up: Use by + time with both tenses. Future perfect = done by then. Future perfect continuous = how long by then.

Reading

Financial experts say everyone should have goals: short-term (1 year), medium-term (2-5 years), and long-term (5+ years). Future perfect shows what will be completed by a future date: "By December, I will have saved $2,400." Future perfect continuous shows how long you will have been doing something by then: "By 2030, I will have been saving for a house for five years."

Start with an emergency fund—three months of expenses. After that, think about bigger goals: a car, a house, education, retirement. Break big goals into smaller milestones. Use by + a future time with both future perfect and future perfect continuous.

1) By the end of this year, what will you have saved? (Future Perfect)
2) What is the difference between "I will have saved" and "I will have been saving"?
3) By 2030, how long will you have been working (or saving for a goal)? (Future Perfect Continuous)

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Describe your financial goals using both future perfect and future perfect continuous. By certain dates, what will you have achieved or saved? How long will you have been saving or working toward a goal by then?

Optional challenge: Use by and by the time with both tenses.
