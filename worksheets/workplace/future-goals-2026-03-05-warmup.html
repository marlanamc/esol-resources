Reading

Financial experts say everyone should have goals: short-term (1 year), medium-term (2-5 years), and long-term (5+ years). We use different future tenses to talk about these goals. "I will save money" (future simple) shows a decision. "I am saving next month" (present continuous) shows a planned action. "I will be saving" (future continuous) shows an ongoing action at a future time. "I will have saved" (future perfect) shows completion by a future date.

Start with an emergency fund—three months of expenses. This protects you if you lose your job or have an unexpected bill. After that, think about bigger goals: a car, a house, education, retirement. Break big goals into smaller milestones. Celebrate each step!
