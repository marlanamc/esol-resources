Part B: Phrases

8 min

Read the examples. Then write your own reflection or advice.

1) I feel more confident about…
  • I feel more confident about speaking at work.
  • I feel more confident about asking questions in English.
Your turn:
2) The hardest thing was…
  • The hardest thing was pronunciation.
  • The hardest thing was understanding fast speakers.
Your turn:
3) The most helpful activity was…
  • The most helpful activity was role-plays.
  • The most helpful activity was practicing with a partner.
Your turn:
4) I recommend… / If you want to improve, you should…
  • I recommend practicing every day, even for 10 minutes.
  • If you want to improve, you should speak as much as possible.
Your turn:
5) I'm proud of…
  • I'm proud of my progress this year.
  • I'm proud of how much my speaking has improved.

Level up: Give a compliment to a classmate: "I noticed that you improved a lot in…"

Part C: Partner Practice

5 min
Round 1: Share your reflections. Partner asks: "What helped you the most?"
Round 2: Switch. Give advice to a new student. Partner asks follow-up questions.

Tips: Be specific. Say WHY something was helpful or difficult.

Reading

Reflecting on your learning helps you see your progress. Think about where you started and where you are now. What improved? What was difficult? What would you do differently?

Sharing advice with new students helps them succeed. What do you wish you knew at the beginning?

1) Why is it important to reflect on your learning?
2) What is one thing you are proud of this year?
3) What advice would you give to a new student?

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Write a letter to a new student giving advice about learning English. Include what helped you and what to expect.
  • You might include at least 3 pieces of advice (I recommend…, You should…)
  • Consider explaining what was helpful or what was challenging
  • Consider sharing one thing you're proud of
  • Try using vocabulary from Part A

Optional: Record yourself reading your letter aloud. How has your speaking improved?
