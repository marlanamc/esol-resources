Part B: Phrases

8 min

Past Perfect = had + past participle (action completed before another past event). Past Perfect Continuous = had been + verb-ing (how long before that past event). Read the examples, then write your own. Use both tenses when you can.

1) I had been ___ (search/look/work) … for ___ when I… (Past Perfect Continuous)
  • I had been searching for a full-time job for three months when I found this position.
  • I had been looking for positions in healthcare for a year when I got hired at the hospital.

2) I had worked as… before I… (Past Perfect)
  • I had worked as a cashier for two years before I got promoted.
  • I had worked as a cleaner before I found a better job.
Your turn:
3) I had applied to… before I… (Past Perfect)
  • I had applied to restaurants and stores before I got an interview.
  • I had applied to many jobs on Indeed before I found this one.
Your turn:
4) I was exhausted because I had been ___ (work/study/practice)… (Past Perfect Continuous—cause)
  • I was exhausted because I had been preparing for interviews all week.
  • She was confident because she had been practicing her answers for months.
Your turn:
5) I had tried to… before I finally… (Past Perfect)
  • I had tried to get an interview at Target before I finally got one.
  • I had tried to improve my computer skills before I applied for this job.

Level up: Connect past events with "before," "when," or "by the time." Use past perfect continuous for how long.

Reading

Looking for a job takes time. Past perfect shows actions completed before another past event: "I had applied to 20 jobs before I got an interview." Past perfect continuous shows how long something had been happening: "I had been searching for six months when I finally got hired."

Both tenses help you tell your job search story in order. Use past perfect (had + V3) for completed actions. Use past perfect continuous (had been + verb-ing) when you want to emphasize duration or the cause of a past situation.

1) What had you done before you started your current job? (Past Perfect)
2) What is the difference between "I had applied" and "I had been applying"?
3) How long had you been looking for a job when you got hired? (Past Perfect Continuous)

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Use your own experience, or imagine: you had been looking for a job for 6 months when you got hired as a cashier. You had applied to 20 jobs, had taken English classes, and had been practicing for interviews with friends. Describe this job search journey using both past perfect and past perfect continuous. What had you done before you found the job? How long had you been searching?

Optional challenge: Use "because" with past perfect continuous to explain a past situation (e.g., "I was tired because I had been applying all day").
