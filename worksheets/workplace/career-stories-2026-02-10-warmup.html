Part B: Phrases

8 min

Past Perfect = had + past participle (action completed before another past event). Past Perfect Continuous = had been + verb-ing (how long before that past event). Read the examples, then write your own. Use both tenses when you can.

1) I had worked as… before I… (Past Perfect)
  • I had worked as a cleaner for three years before I got a new job.
  • I had worked at a factory before I moved to the U.S.
Your turn:
2) I had been ___ (work/look/search) … for ___ when I… (Past Perfect Continuous)
  • I had been looking for a better job for six months when I found this one.
  • I had been working at the restaurant for two years when I decided to change careers.
Your turn:
3) Before I came to the U.S., I had… (Past Perfect)
  • Before I came to the U.S., I had taught elementary school.
  • Before I came to Boston, I had studied computer programming.
Your turn:
4) She was tired because she had been ___ (work/study)… (Past Perfect Continuous—cause)
  • She was tired because she had been working double shifts all week.
  • He was confident because he had been practicing for the interview for months.
Your turn:
5) I had tried to… before I finally… (Past Perfect)
  • I had tried to learn English before I came here.
  • I had tried to find a job with benefits before I got hired at the hospital.
Your turn:

Level up: Connect past events with "before," "when," or "by the time." Use past perfect continuous for how long.

Reading

Everyone has a career story. Past perfect shows an action completed before another past event: "I had worked at a restaurant for two years before I decided to change careers." Past perfect continuous shows how long something had been happening before that event: "I had been looking for a new job for six months when I finally got an interview."

Both tenses help us tell stories in order. Use past perfect for completed actions (had + V3). Use past perfect continuous when you want to emphasize duration (had been + verb-ing).

1) What had you done before you started learning English? (Past Perfect)
2) What is the difference between "I had worked" and "I had been working"?
3) Where had you been living before you moved here? (Past Perfect Continuous)

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Tell your career journey story. What had you done before important changes? How long had you been doing something before a change happened? Use both past perfect and past perfect continuous.

Optional challenge: Use "before," "when," or "because" with both tenses.
