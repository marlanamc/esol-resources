Reading

When an accident happens at work, you need to write an accident report. This report describes what happened, who was involved, and what people said. You use reported speech to tell what witnesses and employees said about the incident.

Accident reports must be accurate and clear. When reporting what someone said, change tenses (present → past, will → would), change pronouns (I → he/she, you → I), and change time words (today → that day, yesterday → the day before). This helps create an official record of what happened.

Writing Homework

Prompt: Imagine you are writing an accident report. At 2pm yesterday, Maria slipped on a wet floor in the kitchen. She told you "I fell because the floor was wet." A witness, Tom, said "I saw her fall." The manager asked "Was anyone injured?" Write the accident report using reported speech. Describe what happened, what witnesses said, and what the injured person told you.

Optional challenge: Include details about time, location, and what witnesses observed. Use different reporting verbs: said, told, asked, explained, mentioned.
