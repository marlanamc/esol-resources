Part C: Partner Practice

5 min
Round 1: Describe a workplace problem using present perfect continuous. Partner asks follow-up questions.
Round 2: Switch. Role-play: Employee explains a problem, manager asks "How long has this been happening?"

Tip: Being specific about duration helps when reporting workplace issues.

Reading

Workers have rights. If you have been experiencing problems at work, it's important to document them. Write down what has been happening and for how long. This helps when you talk to your supervisor or HR.

Present perfect continuous helps explain ongoing problems: "I have been working without breaks for two months" is clearer than "I don't get breaks."

1) Why should you document workplace problems?
2) How is "I have been working without breaks" different from "I don't get breaks"?
3) Who can you talk to about workplace problems?

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Describe a workplace situation (real or imagined) using present perfect continuous. Explain the problem, how long it has been happening, and what you have been doing about it.
  • Try using "have been + -ing"
  • You might include specific time expressions (for X weeks, since January)
  • Consider explaining what you have been doing to resolve the problem
  • Try using workplace vocabulary from Part A

Optional challenge: Compare past simple (yesterday) with present perfect continuous (ongoing).
