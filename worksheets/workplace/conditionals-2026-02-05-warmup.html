Level up: Use "might" or "will probably" to show uncertainty in first conditionals.

Reading

At work, you have responsibilities and deadlines. When you have a deadline, you need to finish your work on time. If you don't meet your deadline, your colleague may be disappointed. If you work hard and do a good job, you might get a promotion or a raise.

Many jobs have schedules. When you follow your schedule, everything runs smoothly. If you arrive late, you might miss important meetings. If you work overtime, you usually get extra pay. Always communicate with your colleagues if you have problems or questions.

1) What happens if you don't meet your deadline? (Zero conditional)
2) What might happen if you work hard and do a good job? (First conditional)
3) What usually happens when you work overtime?

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: What will you do if you get a promotion at work? What will you do if you don't meet an important deadline? Use both zero and first conditionals in your answer.

Optional challenge: Use "unless" (e.g., "I won't work overtime unless it's really necessary").
