Part B: Phrases

8 min

Present Perfect = have/has + past participle (experience, result). Present Perfect Continuous = have/has been + verb-ing (how long, ongoing). Read the examples, then write your own. Try to use vocabulary from Part A.

1) I have worked here for/since… (Present Perfect)
  • I have worked here for three years.
  • I have worked here since 2022.
Your turn:
2) I have been learning… (Present Perfect Continuous)
  • I have been learning the new computer system.
  • I have been studying English since January.
Your turn:
3) I have already… / I haven't… yet (Present Perfect)
  • I have already finished the report.
  • I haven't called in sick yet this year.
Your turn:
4) She has been training… (Present Perfect Continuous)
  • She has been training new employees all week.
  • He has been improving his skills for months.
Your turn:
5) Have you ever…? (Present Perfect)
  • Have you ever had a promotion?
  • Have you ever worked overtime?
Your turn:

Level up: Use for and since with both tenses. Use recently or so far.

Reading

Maria has worked at the hospital for five years. She has been training new nursing assistants since January. So far this year, she has trained six people. She has never missed a day of work. Her supervisor has been talking about a promotion. Maria has been improving her English; she has taken two ESOL classes already.

At work, we often use present perfect to talk about experience and what we have or have not done. We use present perfect continuous to emphasize how long we have been doing something.

1) How long has Maria worked at the hospital? (Present Perfect)
2) What has Maria been doing since January? (Present Perfect Continuous)
3) What has Maria already done? Name one thing.

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: What have you accomplished at work (or in your job search)? How long have you been doing your current job or learning new skills? Use both present perfect and present perfect continuous. Use for, since, already, yet, or so far when you can.

Optional challenge: Use "Have you ever…?" and answer with present perfect.
