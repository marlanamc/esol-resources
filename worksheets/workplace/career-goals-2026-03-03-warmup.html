Part B: Phrases

8 min

Future Perfect = will have + past participle (completed by a future time). Future Perfect Continuous = will have been + verb-ing (how long by that future time). Read the examples, then write your own. Use both tenses when you can.

1) By next year, I will have… (Future Perfect)
  • By next year, I will have finished my training program.
  • By next year, I will have saved enough money for my goal.

2) By December, I will have been ___ (work/study) … for ___ (Future Perfect Continuous)
  • By December, I will have been working at this company for five years.
  • By December, I will have been studying English for three years.

3) In two years, I will have… (Future Perfect)
  • In two years, I will have achieved my career goal.
  • In two years, I will have graduated from my program.

4) By the time I [achieve goal], I will have… (Future Perfect)
  • By the time I become a manager, I will have worked in retail for five years.
  • By the time I graduate, I will have completed all my classes.

5) By 2030, I will have been ___ (work/live/study) … for ___ (Future Perfect Continuous)
  • By 2030, I will have been working here for a decade.
  • By 2030, I will have been living in Boston for 10 years.

Level up: Use by + time with both tenses. Future perfect = done by then. Future perfect continuous = how long by then.

Reading

Long-term goals help you plan your future. Future perfect shows what will be completed by a future point: "By 2030, I will have retired." Future perfect continuous shows how long something will have been happening by that point: "By 2030, I will have been working at this company for ten years."

Both tenses use by + a future time. Use future perfect for completed actions (will have + V3). Use future perfect continuous when you want to emphasize duration (will have been + verb-ing).

1) By the end of this year, what will you have completed? (Future Perfect)
2) What is the difference between "I will have worked" and "I will have been working"?
3) By next year, how long will you have been studying English? (Future Perfect Continuous)
