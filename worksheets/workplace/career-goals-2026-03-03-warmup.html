Part B: Quick Talk Frames

8 min

Present Perfect = have/has + V3 (life experience or result now). Present Perfect Continuous = have/has been + verb-ing (activity started in the past and continuing now).

1) I have already ___ (V3) ... (Present Perfect)
  • I have already completed one certificate class.
  • I have already applied for two better jobs.
Your turn:
2) I have been ___ing ... for/since ... (Present Perfect Continuous)
  • I have been studying English for two years.
  • I have been working on my resume since January.
Your turn:
3) Have you ever ___ (V3) ... ? (Present Perfect question)
  • Have you ever asked for a promotion?
  • Have you ever changed careers?
Your turn:
4) How long have you been ___ing ... ? (Present Perfect Continuous question)
  • How long have you been working at your current job?
  • How long have you been learning this skill?
Your turn:
5) Recently, I have ___ (V3) ..., and I have been ___ing ...
  • Recently, I have set a new goal, and I have been practicing interview answers.
  • Recently, I have achieved one milestone, and I have been building better work habits.
Your turn:

Level up: Try to use already, yet, ever, since, and for.

Personal Career Check-In

10 min

Use the sample row as a guide, then fill in your own goals. Your goals can be about work, English learning, health, personal life, money, family, or another important area. Keep answers short.

My goal What I have done (Present Perfect) What I have been doing (Present Perfect Continuous) Next step this week
Sample: become a bartender (now I am a server) I have asked my manager about bartender training. I have been learning drink names and bar steps since January. Ask to shadow the bartender one shift this week.

Optional challenge: Use one irregular verb correctly (for example: done, written, spoken, grown).
