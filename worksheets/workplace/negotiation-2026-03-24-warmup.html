Level up: Remember: Second Conditional = if + past simple, would + base verb (unreal present/future). Third Conditional = if + past perfect, would have + past participle (unreal past).

Reading

Conditionals help us talk about advice, risks, and reflection. Second Conditional (if + past simple, would + base verb) talks about unreal situations in the present or future: "If I were you, I would ask for advice." This gives advice about hypothetical situations.

Third Conditional (if + past perfect, would have + past participle) talks about unreal situations in the past: "If I had known the risks, I would have made a different decision." This reflects on past choices and their consequences.

1) When do we use Second Conditional versus Third Conditional?
2) What is the difference between "If I were you, I would ask for advice" and "If I had asked for advice, I would have avoided the problem"?
3) How can conditionals help you reflect on past decisions and give advice about future risks?

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Imagine a coworker is considering asking for a raise but is nervous. They have been working at the company for 2 years and have never asked for more money. Write about workplace advice using Second and Third Conditional. Give advice about this situation (what would happen if they asked?), or reflect on a past decision (what would you have done if you had been in their situation?).

Optional challenge: Mix Second and Third Conditional in your writing to give advice and reflect on past experiences.
