Part B: Phrases

8 min

Read the examples. Practice mixing infinitives and gerunds for workplace advocacy.

1) I'd like to discuss… (infinitive)
  • I'd like to discuss my schedule.
  • I'd like to discuss the new policy with you.
Your turn:
2) I enjoy…, but I need to… (gerund + infinitive)
  • I enjoy working here, but I need to have consistent hours.
  • I enjoy helping customers, but I need to take my breaks.
Your turn:
3) Would you consider…? (gerund)
  • Would you consider giving me more notice about schedule changes?
  • Would you consider changing my shift to mornings?
Your turn:
4) I suggest… (gerund)
  • I suggest meeting weekly to discuss this.
  • I suggest reviewing the policy together.
Your turn:
5) I plan to… / I agree to… (infinitive)
  • I plan to document everything in writing.
  • I agree to follow the new policy if we can compromise on hours.
Your turn:

Level up: Use "avoid" + gerund: "I want to avoid missing any more shifts."

Part C: Partner Practice

5 min
Round 1: Role-play: Employee explains a problem using infinitives (I need to…, I want to…).
Round 2: Switch. Manager asks "Would you consider…?" Employee responds with both what they enjoy AND what they need.

Tip: Stay calm and polite. Explain both the problem and a possible solution.

Reading

Self-advocacy means speaking up for yourself professionally. When you advocate, stay calm and use polite language. Mix infinitives (I'd like to, I need to, I plan to) with gerunds (I enjoy, I suggest, Would you consider).

Good advocacy includes stating the problem, offering a solution, and being willing to compromise.

1) What does self-advocacy mean?
2) Name two things good advocacy includes.
3) Why is it important to stay calm when advocating?

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Write a workplace advocacy conversation. Include the employee's request, the manager's response, and the agreement they reach.
  • Try using infinitives (I need to, I agree to, I plan to)
  • Try using gerunds (I enjoy, I suggest, Would you consider)
  • You might include a polite problem statement or a compromise
  • Document the agreement: "We agreed to…"

Optional challenge: Use "keep" + gerund (I will keep documenting) and "avoid" + gerund (I will avoid missing shifts).
