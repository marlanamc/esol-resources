Reading

When applying for jobs, you need to describe your skills and qualifications. Certain verbs are always followed by gerunds: enjoy, avoid, finish, consider, and keep. For example, "I enjoy working with people" or "I finished taking my training course." Other verbs are always followed by infinitives: want, plan, hope, need, decide, and refuse. For example, "I want to get certified" or "I decided to apply for the job."

Some verbs can take both gerunds and infinitives, but the meaning changes. "I stopped working" means I quit my job. "I stopped to work" means I paused what I was doing in order to work. "I remember getting my certificate" recalls a past action. "I remember to get my certificate" means I don't forget a future action.

When talking about jobs and skills, use gerunds and infinitives correctly. Employers want to know what you enjoy doing, what you want to learn, and what qualifications you need to get. Practice using these patterns when describing your work experience and career goals.
