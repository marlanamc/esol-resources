Level up: Use for and since with present perfect continuous when talking about your job search.

Reading

A job application talk-through helps you practice explaining your experience. Use present perfect to say what you have done: "I have applied to ten jobs." "I have had two interviews." Use present perfect continuous to say how long you have been doing something: "I have been looking for a job for three months." "I have been updating my resume since last week."

When you talk through your application with a friend or in class, use both tenses. Say what you have done so far and how long you have been searching or preparing. This helps you sound clear and confident in an interview.

1) How long have you been looking for a job? (Present Perfect Continuous)
2) What have you already done for your job search? Name two things. (Present Perfect)
3) What is the difference between "I have applied" and "I have been applying"?

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Talk through your job application experience. How long have you been looking? What have you applied to? What have you already done? What haven't you done yet? Use both present perfect and present perfect continuous. Use for, since, already, yet, or so far.

Optional challenge: Use "Have you ever had an interview?" and answer with present perfect.
