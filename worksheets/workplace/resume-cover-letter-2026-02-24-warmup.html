Part B: Phrases

8 min

Read the examples. Then write your own sentence. Practice using modals.

1) I must include…
  • I must include my contact information at the top of my resume.
  • I must include my work experience in reverse order.
Your turn:
2) You should list…
  • You should list your most recent job first.
  • You should list skills that match the job description.
Your turn:
3) Could you please…?
  • Could you please give me feedback on my cover letter?
  • Could you please check my resume for mistakes?
Your turn:
4) May I use you as a reference?
  • May I use you as a reference for this job application?
  • May I list your phone number on my resume?
Your turn:
5) I have to submit…
  • I have to submit my application by Friday.
  • I have to submit three references with my resume.
Your turn:

Level up: Vary the strength—use "must" (strong), "should" (medium), and "could" (soft suggestion).

Part C: Partner Practice

5 min
Round 1: Role-play: Ask your partner to review your resume. Use polite modals (Can you…? Could you…? Would you…?).
Round 2: Switch. Partner gives feedback using modals (You should…, You could…, You must…).

Tips: Make eye contact and thank them for their time.

Reading

A resume shows your work history, skills, and education. A cover letter explains why you want the job. You must include accurate contact information. You should list your experience with the most recent job first.

Before you submit your application, ask someone to review it. They may find mistakes you missed. Using references? You must ask permission first.

1) What is the difference between a resume and a cover letter?
2) What must you do before using someone as a reference?
3) Why should you ask someone to review your resume?

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Explain what you must include, should include, and could include on a resume. Give advice to someone writing their first resume.
  • Try using "must" for requirements (contact info, work history)
  • Try using "should" for strong suggestions (skills, formatting)
  • Try using "could" or "may" for optional items (references, hobbies)
  • Try writing in complete sentences

Optional challenge: Write a short cover letter paragraph using modals.
