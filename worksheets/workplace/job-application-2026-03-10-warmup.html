Part C: Partner Practice

5 min
Round 1: Ask: "How long have you been looking for a job?" or "What have you been doing to find work?"
Round 2: Switch. Ask: "What challenges have you been facing in your job search?"

Remember: have been + -ing = ongoing action that started in the past and continues.

Reading

Looking for a job takes time. Many people have been searching for months before they get hired. It's important to keep applying and to improve your skills while you search.

Present perfect continuous shows ongoing actions. "I have been working" means you started in the past and still continue. "I have been looking for a job for two months" shows the duration of your search.

1) How long have you been studying English?
2) What is the difference between "I worked" and "I have been working"?
3) What have you been doing to prepare for your next job?

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Describe your job search journey using present perfect continuous. What have you been doing? How long have you been searching?
  • Try using "I have been + -ing"
  • You might include time expressions (for X months, since January)
  • You could describe what you have been doing to find work
  • Try mentioning any challenges you have been facing

Optional challenge: Compare past simple (I worked) with present perfect continuous (I have been working).
