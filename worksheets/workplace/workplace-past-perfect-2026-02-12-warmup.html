Part B: Phrases

8 min

Past Perfect = had + past participle (something happened before another past action). Use it for work timelines: "I had already left when they called." Read the examples, then write your own.

1) I had already ___ when… (Past Perfect + Past Simple)
  • I had already left the office when my boss called.
  • She had already applied when they closed the job.
Your turn:
2) By the time I ___, I had ___ (Past Perfect = before that moment)
  • By the time I got the job, I had had three interviews.
  • By the time she graduated, she had worked at two companies.
Your turn:
3) I had never ___ before I ___ (Past Perfect + Past Simple)
  • I had never had a promotion before I joined this company.
  • He had never worked overtime before he started this job.
Your turn:
4) When ___, they had already ___ (Past Perfect = already done before)
  • When I arrived, the meeting had already started.
  • When she applied, they had already hired someone.
Your turn:

Level up: Past perfect = had + past participle. It always goes with another past action (past simple).

Reading

Maria got a promotion last month. By the time she got the promotion, she had worked at the hospital for five years. She had already trained six new assistants. She had never missed a day of work. When the manager called to offer the job, Maria had already left for the day, so she called back the next morning.

We use past perfect (had + past participle) to show that one action happened before another action in the past. It helps tell a clear timeline at work.

1) What had Maria done by the time she got the promotion? (Past Perfect)
2) When the manager called, had Maria left already? (Past Perfect)
3) Write one sentence about your work or life using past perfect (had + past participle) and past simple.

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Tell a short work or life timeline. What had already happened before something else? Use past perfect (had + past participle) with past simple. Example: "By the time I got my first job, I had already finished school." Use by the time, when, already, or before when you can.

Optional challenge: Write two events in order using "I had already ___ when ___."
