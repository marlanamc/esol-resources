Reading

Rosa works at a warehouse. She works the morning shift from 7:00 a.m. to 3:00 p.m. Every Monday, her supervisor meets with the team to go over the safety policy. If there is a problem, Rosa emails her supervisor and promises to follow up later with more information.

Last month, Rosa noticed a broken step near the loading dock. She told her supervisor right away and sent a photo by email. Her supervisor said she would check with the maintenance team and asked Rosa to follow up with her the next day. The step was fixed within two days. Rosa was glad she spoke up.

At the end of each week, Rosa checks her hours and fills out her timesheet. Before she leaves, she turns in the timesheet to the office. The company policy says all timesheets must be submitted by Friday at 4:00 p.m. Rosa likes to finish hers early so she never forgets.

Sometimes Rosa's shift changes when the warehouse is very busy. When that happens, her supervisor sends an email or talks to her in person to explain the new schedule. Clear policies, helpful supervisors, and good communication help Rosa feel confident at work.

1) Why might Rosa promise to "follow up later with more information" when she emails about a problem? What does that show about good workplace communication?
2) The passage says clear policies help Rosa feel confident. Give one reason why knowing the rules might make someone feel more confident at work.
3) Describe one way your work or school routine is similar to Rosa's, or one way it is different.
