Remember: had been + -ing = ongoing action that was happening BEFORE another past event.

Reading

Everyone has a career story. Before you started your current job, what had you been doing? Past perfect continuous helps us tell these stories. It shows an ongoing action that was happening before another past event.

For example: "I had been working at a restaurant for two years before I decided to change careers." This shows the duration of the first action before the change.

1) What had you been doing before you started learning English?
2) What is the difference between "I worked" and "I had been working"?
3) How long had you been living in your country before you moved?

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Tell your career journey story. What had you been doing before important changes in your life?
  • Try using "I had been + -ing"
  • You might include time expressions (for X years, before I moved)
  • You could describe at least two important changes in your career
  • Connect events with "before," "when," or "by the time"

Optional challenge: Compare present perfect continuous (have been working) with past perfect continuous (had been working).
