Reading

When you describe your home, it helps to use location words. Words like "next to," "across from," "behind," and "between" help people understand where things are. For example: "The bathroom is next to my bedroom" is clearer than "The bathroom is near my room."

Adjectives also make your description better. Instead of "My room is OK," try "My room is small but bright" or "My room is quiet and comfortable." These details help people picture your home.

2) Why is "next to" clearer than "near"?
3) Write two adjectives that describe your home.

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Describe your home in detail. What rooms do you have? What furniture is in each room? What do you like about your home?
  • You could name the rooms in your home (use "There is/There are")
  • You could describe the furniture in at least 2 rooms
  • Try using location words (next to, across from, between)
  • Try using adjectives to describe your home (bright, quiet, spacious, small)

Optional challenge: Compare your home now to a home you lived in before (use "bigger than," "smaller than," "more quiet").
