Reading

If you have a problem in your apartment, tell your landlord first. Write a letter or email so you have a record. If the landlord doesn't fix it, you have options. You can file a complaint with your city's housing department. An inspector can visit and check the problem.

Always document problems: take photos, write dates, and keep copies of letters. This information helps if you need to take legal action. Know your rights as a tenant!

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Imagine your heating stopped working last week during cold weather. The apartment has been very cold, and you tried to call your landlord, but they didn't answer. Describe the problem, what you tried to do, and what the solution was (or what you think should happen).

Optional challenge: Give advice to someone with the same problem (use "You should…" or "I recommend…").
