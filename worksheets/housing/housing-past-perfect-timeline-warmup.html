Part B: Phrases

Past Perfect = had + past participle (something happened before another past action). Use it for housing timelines. Read the examples, then write your own.

1) By the time I ___, the landlord had ___ (Past Perfect + Past Simple)
  • By the time I called, the landlord had already fixed the heat.
  • By the time I moved in, the landlord had repaired the window.

Level up: Past perfect = had + past participle. It always goes with another past action (past simple) to show order.

Reading

When you have a housing problem, the order of events matters. Past perfect (had + past participle) shows what happened before another past action. "By the time I called, the landlord had already fixed the heat" means the repair was done before your call. "I had already sent the complaint when the landlord called" means you sent it first.

Use past perfect to tell a clear timeline: what had already happened, and what happened next. This helps when you explain repairs, complaints, or move-in dates.
