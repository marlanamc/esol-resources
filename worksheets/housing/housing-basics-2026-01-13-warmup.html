Reading

In many cities, rent costs more now. Many families must make hard choices. Some people live with roommates. Some move farther from the subway. Some share bedrooms. When you look for housing, think about your priorities: price, location, safety, and space.

Ask clear questions before you sign a lease. For example: What utilities are included? Is heat included? How long is the lease? What is the policy for repairs? Asking questions can prevent problems later.

1) Name two ways people save money on housing.
2) What are two priorities you think about when choosing housing?
3) Write one question you should ask before signing a lease.

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Describe your home now. Then describe your ideal home. What is different? Why do you want the change?

Optional challenge: Use present perfect (I've lived…) and a contrast phrase (even though, however).
