Reading

When you rent an apartment, you sign a lease. The lease is a contract. It says how long you will stay and how much you will pay. If you break the lease early, you may have to pay a fee. If you want to move out, you usually need to give 30 days notice.

At the end of your lease, you can renew it or move out. If the landlord raises the rent, you can negotiate or look for a new place. Always read your lease carefully so you know the rules.

1) What happens if you break your lease early?
2) How much notice do you usually need to give before moving out?
3) What are your two choices at the end of a lease?

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: What will you do if your rent goes up next year? What are your options? What would you choose?
  • Try using at least 3 "if" sentences
  • Consider explaining your options (stay, move, get roommate, etc.)
  • Try to say what you would probably do or why
  • Try using vocabulary from Part A

Optional challenge: Use "unless" (e.g., "I won't move unless the rent goes up a lot").
