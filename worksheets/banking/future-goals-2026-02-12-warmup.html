Reading

Financial experts say everyone should have goals: short-term (1 year), medium-term (2-5 years), and long-term (5+ years). Start with an emergency fund—three months of expenses. This protects you if you lose your job or have an unexpected bill.

After that, think about bigger goals: a car, a house, education, retirement. Break big goals into smaller milestones. Celebrate each step!

1) What is the difference between short-term and long-term goals?
2) How much should you have in an emergency fund?
3) Why should you break big goals into milestones?

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Describe your financial goals. What do you want to achieve in 1 year? In 5 years? What steps will you take?
  • Consider sharing one short-term or one long-term goal
  • Consider explaining why these goals are important to you
  • You could describe milestones or steps to reach your goal
  • Try using vocabulary from Part A

Optional challenge: Use future perfect (e.g., "By 2030, I will have saved…").
