Reading

Banks offer different types of accounts. A checking account is for everyday spending. You can use a debit card or write checks. A savings account is for saving money. It earns a little interest, but you shouldn't use it every day.

Be careful with fees! Many banks charge fees for low balances, overdrafts, or using another bank's ATM. Read your bank's rules so you don't get surprised.

1) What is the difference between checking and savings accounts?
2) Name two things banks charge fees for.
3) How can you avoid fees at your bank?

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Describe your banking habits. What accounts do you have? How do you pay bills? What fees have you paid?

Optional challenge: Compare your bank to another bank (e.g., "My bank has lower fees than…").
