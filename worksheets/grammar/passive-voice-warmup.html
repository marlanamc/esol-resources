Reading

Passive voice is useful when the action is more important than who does it, or when the doer is unknown or obvious. We use passive voice for processes: "Coffee is grown in the mountains, then the beans are roasted and ground." We also use it for official announcements: "The meeting has been canceled" or "Your application was received."

Passive voice is formed with a form of "be" + past participle. Simple past: "The house was built in 1990." Present perfect: "The road has been repaired." Present continuous: "The building is being renovated right now." Future: "The project will be completed next month."

In everyday conversation, active voice is more common. However, passive voice appears frequently in formal writing, news reports, scientific descriptions, and instructions. Recognizing and using passive voice appropriately makes your English more sophisticated.

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Use passive voice to describe things in your life or community. Where were you born? What was built in your neighborhood? What changes have been made recently? What is being renovated or repaired?
  • Try using simple past passive ("was built," "was founded")
  • Consider using present perfect passive ("has been renovated," "have been completed")
  • Try using present continuous passive ("is being repaired," "are being built")
  • Could describe both historical facts and current changes

Optional challenge: Use future passive voice ("will be completed," "will have been built by...").
