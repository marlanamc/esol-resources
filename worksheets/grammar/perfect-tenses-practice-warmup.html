Reading

Past continuous describes background actions that were in progress: "I was cooking dinner." Past simple describes completed actions or interruptions: "The phone rang." When you combine them, past continuous gives context, and past simple tells what happened: "While I was cooking dinner, the phone rang."

Use "when" with past simple for the interruption: "When the fire alarm went off, I was sleeping." Use "while" with past continuous for the background action: "While I was waiting for the bus, it started raining." You can also describe two simultaneous background actions: "While I was studying, my roommate was cooking."

Past continuous is also useful for describing what was happening at a specific time: "At 7am this morning, I was getting ready for work." This helps paint a picture of your day or explain your schedule. Combining these two tenses makes your storytelling more vivid and detailed.
