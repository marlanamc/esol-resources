Reading

Perfect continuous tenses emphasize the duration and ongoing nature of actions. Present perfect continuous shows an action that started in the past and continues now: "I have been studying English for two years" (and I still am). Use "for" with duration (for two years) and "since" with starting points (since 2022).

Past perfect continuous shows an action that was ongoing before another past event: "I had been working there for five years when I got promoted." This emphasizes how long the action continued before something else happened. It's useful for explaining background or context in stories.

Future perfect continuous projects ongoing duration into the future: "By 2025, I will have been living in Boston for three years." This structure is less common but useful for milestones and achievements. All perfect continuous tenses work best with time expressions to show exactly how long something has been, had been, or will have been happening.

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Use perfect continuous tenses to describe your experiences. What have you been doing lately? How long have you been studying English or working at your job? Think of a time you were exhausted—what had you been doing? What goals will you have been pursuing by next year?
  • Try using present perfect continuous with "for" or "since"
  • Consider using past perfect continuous to explain background before a past event
  • Try using future perfect continuous to describe a milestone
  • Could include specific time durations to emphasize how long

Optional challenge: Combine perfect continuous with other tenses in a story (e.g., "I had been studying for hours when the power went out").
