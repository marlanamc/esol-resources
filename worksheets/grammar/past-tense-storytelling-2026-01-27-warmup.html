Part C: Partner Practice

5 min
Round 1: Share your sentences from Part B. Your partner listens and asks: "What happened next?" or "What were you doing before that?"
Round 2: Switch roles. Now tell your partner about one time something interrupted your day. Use "I was [verb]-ing when..." and "While I was [verb]-ing..." in your story.

Need help starting? Say: "Last week, I was [verb]-ing when suddenly..." or "Yesterday, while I was [verb]-ing, ..."

Reading

Good stories use both past simple and past continuous. Past simple tells what happened (finished actions). Past continuous tells what was happening (background, actions in progress).

For example: "I was driving to work when I saw the accident. The car was going too fast. It hit a tree and stopped suddenly." This story shows background (was driving, was going) and main events (saw, hit, stopped).

1) What is the difference between "I walked" and "I was walking"?
2) Do you use "when" or "while" with past continuous?
3) Think of one time something unexpected interrupted your day. What were you doing?

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Write about a time when something unexpected happened to you. Tell the complete story.
  • Use at least 6 sentences
  • Use past simple AND past continuous at least 2 times each
  • Use at least 2 time expressions from Part A (meanwhile, suddenly, before, while, when)
  • Describe: What were you doing? What happened? What did you do next?

Optional challenge: Use "when" with past simple and "while" with past continuous in the same sentence.
