Reading

These three structures look similar but have different meanings. 'Used to' describes past habits that don't happen anymore: 'I used to smoke' (but I quit). 'Be used to' describes what you are accustomed to now: 'I'm used to waking up early' (it's comfortable for me). 'Get used to' describes the process of becoming accustomed: 'I'm getting used to the food here' (still adjusting).

'Used to' is followed by the base verb: 'I used to work there.' 'Be used to' and 'get used to' are followed by nouns or gerunds: 'I'm used to working nights' or 'I'm used to the noise.' Don't confuse these with 'get to,' which means having the opportunity: 'I get to see my family this weekend' (I'm lucky to have this chance).

These structures are especially useful when talking about major life changes like moving to a new country, starting a new job, or changing routines. They help you explain your journey from past habits through current adjustment to future comfort.

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Describe changes in your life using all three structures. What did you use to do in your home country? What are you used to now? What are you getting used to? What can't you get used to?
  • Try using "used to" to describe at least one past habit
  • Consider using "be used to" to describe something you're comfortable with now
  • Try using "getting used to" to describe an ongoing adjustment
  • Could explain the progression from past to present to show your journey

Optional challenge: Compare then and now in detail (e.g., "I used to eat rice for breakfast, but now I'm used to eating cereal").
