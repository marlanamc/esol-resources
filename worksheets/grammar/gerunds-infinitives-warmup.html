Reading

Knowing when to use gerunds versus infinitives is an important English skill. Certain verbs are always followed by gerunds: enjoy, avoid, quit, finish, consider, and keep. For example, "I enjoy cooking" or "I quit smoking." Other verbs are always followed by infinitives: want, plan, hope, need, decide, and refuse. For example, "I want to learn" or "I decided to move."

Some verbs can take both gerunds and infinitives, but the meaning changes slightly. "I stopped smoking" means I quit the habit. "I stopped to smoke" means I paused what I was doing in order to smoke. "I remember doing homework" recalls a past action. "I remember to do homework" means I don't forget a future action.

The best way to learn this is through practice and exposure. Pay attention to which verbs your teacher and classmates use with gerunds or infinitives. Over time, these patterns will become natural and automatic.

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Write about your goals and interests using both gerunds and infinitives. What do you enjoy doing? What have you stopped or started doing? What do you want to achieve? What are you considering trying?
  • Try using at least 3 verbs with gerunds (enjoy, avoid, quit, finish, keep, consider)
  • Consider using at least 3 verbs with infinitives (want, plan, hope, need, decide, refuse)
  • Try mixing both in your sentences naturally
  • Could explain why you made certain decisions or changes

Optional challenge: Use "remember doing" (past) versus "remember to do" (future) correctly in your writing.
