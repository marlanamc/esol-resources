Guess-the-Why Cheat Sheet

Follow-Up Questions + Sentence Frames (Present Perfect Family)
Goal: discover the hidden reason. Ask clear follow-up questions. Answer with have/has been + -ing. Add one present perfect result sentence when possible.

Follow-Up Starters

  • What have you been doing?
  • How long have you been doing that?
  • Since when have you been doing that?
  • Where have you been doing that?
  • Who have you been doing that with?
  • Have you been doing this all day?
  • Have you been doing this at work?
  • Have you been doing this outside?

Guess Questions

  • Have you been ___ing because of the snow?
  • Have you been working extra shifts?
  • Have you been carrying heavy things?
  • Have you been standing for a long time?
  • Have you been shouting over loud machines?
  • Have you been calling out orders in the kitchen?
  • Have you been clearing snow at work?
  • Have you been waiting for transportation in the cold?

Answer Frames (Student A)

  • I have been ___ing all day.
  • I have been ___ing for ___.
  • I have been ___ing since ___.
  • Lately, I have been ___ing a lot.
  • Yes, I have been ___ing.
  • No, I have not been ___ing.
  • I have been ___ing at work.
  • I have been ___ing because ___.

Result Add-On Frames

  • So far, I have ___.
  • I have already ___.
  • I have not ___ yet.
  • I have finished ___.
  • I have lost my voice.
  • I have used my whole water bottle.
  • I have taken a short break.
  • I have sent a message to my supervisor.
Quick Model
A: My voice is gone.
B: What have you been doing?
A: I have been talking loudly at work.
B: How long have you been doing that?
A: I have been doing that since 7 AM.
B: Have you been shouting over loud machines all shift?
A: Yes, I have been shouting over loud machines all shift.
A (result): I have already finished my whole water bottle.
