Reading

Boston has many grocery stores, and each one has different advantages. Market Basket is the cheapest option for most items. Stop & Shop is convenient because there are many locations. Whole Foods is the most expensive, but it has the best organic produce. Trader Joe's has the most unique items you can't find anywhere else.

When shopping, it's important to use quantifiers correctly. "How much does it cost?" is for uncountable nouns like rice or milk. "How many apples do you want?" is for countable nouns. Some people spend too much money on groceries because they don't plan. Others don't buy enough healthy food.

Superlatives help you make comparisons. The fastest restaurant might not be the healthiest. The cheapest option might not have enough variety. Finding the best balance for your budget and health is important.

1) According to the reading, which Boston grocery store is the cheapest?
2) What is the difference between "how much" and "how many"?
3) Why might the cheapest grocery option not be the best choice for everyone?

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Compare three grocery stores or restaurants you know. Which is the best? The cheapest? The most convenient? What do you buy too much of? What do you not buy enough of?

Optional challenge: Include prices or specific examples to support your comparisons.
