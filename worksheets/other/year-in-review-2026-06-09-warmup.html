Reading

Reflecting on your English learning journey helps you see your progress. At the beginning of the year, you might have felt nervous speaking English. Now, you've improved in many ways. Maybe you're more confident, your pronunciation is better, or you can understand more vocabulary.

Thinking about the most useful units helps you remember important skills. For some students, the Housing unit was most useful because they learned how to talk to landlords. For others, the Workplace unit taught them how to advocate for themselves at work. The Healthcare unit helped people communicate with doctors.

Looking ahead is also important. What do you want to continue practicing? What challenges do you still face? Setting new goals keeps you motivated. Remember: learning English is a journey, not a race. Every small improvement matters.

1) Why is reflecting on your learning journey helpful?
2) What are three examples of units that students found most useful?
3) What does the reading say about English learning?

Writing Homework

6–8 sentences
Prompt: Reflect on your year in ESOL class. What was the most useful unit? What is your favorite phrase that you actually used? What have you improved in? What do you want to continue working on?
  • Try explaining which unit was most useful and why
  • Consider sharing a phrase you used in real life
  • Try describing your improvement using present perfect
  • Could set a goal for what to practice next

Optional challenge: Give advice to a new Level 3 student about what helped you succeed.
