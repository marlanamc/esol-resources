Guess-the-Why Detective Cards

Present Perfect Family (ESOL Level 3) • Hard Mode • Workplace-Safe
Student A draws one card. A reads only the READ THIS line. A keeps the DON'T READ OUT LOUD line hidden until Student B guesses it.
  • No reveal rule: A cannot read the secret why sentence until B guesses it or asks 4+ questions.
  • Grammar rule: A answers with have/has been + -ing.
  • Question rule: B asks at least one What have you been doing? and one How long...? question.
  • Scoring: B gets 2 points if guessed in 3 questions, 1 point in 4-5 questions. A gets 1 point per accurate present perfect continuous answer.
Model Round (Scaffold Example)
Condition card (visible): My voice is gone.
Secret why (hidden): I have been shouting over loud machines all shift at the factory.
A: My voice is gone.
B: What have you been doing?
A: I have been talking loudly all day at work.
B: How long have you been doing that?
A: I have been doing it since 8 AM.
B: Have you been working in a noisy place?
A: Yes, I have been working in a very noisy place.
B: Have you been shouting over loud machines all shift at the factory?
A: Yes! I have been shouting over loud machines all shift at the factory.
Optional result sentence: I have already finished my whole water bottle.
Sentence frames: I have been ___ing for ___. / I have been ___ing since ___. / Yes, I have been ___ing all day.

Follow-Up Question Cards (Set C)

Follow-Up
What have you been doing?
Start broad.
Follow-Up
How long have you been doing that?
Use for + duration.
Follow-Up
Since when have you been doing that?
Use since + start time.
Follow-Up
Where have you been doing that?
Ask for place clues.
Follow-Up
Who have you been doing that with?
Ask for people clues.
Follow-Up
Have you been doing this all day?
Yes/no check.
Follow-Up
Have you been doing this at work?
Context check.
Follow-Up
Have you been doing this with children?
Narrowing guess question.
Follow-Up
Have you been doing this since this morning?
Time narrowing.
Follow-Up
Have you been ______ing because ______?
Final guess pattern.
