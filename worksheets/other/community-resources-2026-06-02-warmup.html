Reading

Every community has resources that can help residents. Libraries offer free classes, computer access, and English conversation groups. Community health centers provide affordable medical care. Career centers help with resumes and job searches. Food pantries are available for people who need groceries.

When you recommend a resource, be specific. Explain what it offers, where it is, and what documents to bring. For example: "The community health center is on Main Street. It can help you with medical care. You should bring your ID, proof of address, and insurance card if you have one."

Knowing how to give directions is also important. Use clear steps: "First, take the Red Line to Park Street. Then, transfer to the Green Line. Get off at Copley Station. The building is across the street from the library." Clear directions help people feel confident trying new places.
