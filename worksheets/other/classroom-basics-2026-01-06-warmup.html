Reading

In an ESOL classroom, it's important to ask for help when you don't understand. Good students aren't afraid to say "I don't understand" or "Could you repeat that?" These phrases show that you want to learn.

Being polite is important in American classrooms. Use phrases like "Excuse me" before asking a question. Say "Could you please..." instead of just "Repeat." If the teacher is speaking too fast, it's okay to say "Can you say that more slowly?"

Learning how to ask for clarification is a valuable skill. It helps you in class, at work, and in daily life. Don't be embarrassed—asking questions shows confidence and a desire to improve your English.
