Reading

Giving a final presentation is a chance to share what you've learned this year. A good presentation should be organized: start with your goal, share what you learned, give one specific example, and explain your next steps. Keeping it to 60-90 seconds ensures you stay focused on the most important points.

Adding specific details makes your presentation stronger. Instead of "I learned English," say "I learned 200 new vocabulary words and improved my pronunciation." Instead of "I used it at work," say "I used these phrases when I talked to my manager about my schedule." Details show that you really understand and apply what you learned.

Practice is important. Rehearse with a partner and ask for feedback. They might suggest speaking louder, slowing down, or adding an example. Remember: making mistakes during practice helps you improve before the final presentation. Be proud of how far you've come this year!
