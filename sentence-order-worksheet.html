Sentence Order Worksheet

Type: Worksheet Level: Intermediate

Sentence Order: Who + Verb + What + Where + When

Quick Review

English sentences usually follow this order:

WHO + VERB + WHAT + WHERE + WHEN

Important Rules:

  • WHO + VERB always stay together. You can move WHEN or WHERE, but do not put them between WHO and VERB.
  • WHAT comes directly after the verb most of the time.
  • WHEN usually comes at the end, unless you move it to the beginning.
  • WHERE and WHEN can go before WHO, but you must add a comma after them when they're at the beginning.
  • WHERE is optional - you don't always need both WHERE and WHEN in every sentence.

Understanding Each Part

WHO Subject of the sentence

WHO is the subject - the person or thing doing the action. It can be:

  • Pronoun - I, you, he, she, they, we
  • Possessive + Noun - my friend, your teacher, his sister
  • Article + Noun - a student, an apple, the teacher
  • Noun - John, Maria, students

Examples: She, My friend, The students

VERB Action or state

VERB is the action or state. It can be:

  • V1 / V1-3rd alone - eat/eats, work/works
  • V2 alone - ate, worked, went
  • Helper + Verb - do/does/did/will + eat
  • To Be + V-ing - am/is/are + eating
  • Have/Has/Had + V3 - has + eaten
  • Have/Has/Had + been + V-ing - has been eating

Examples: eats, is studying, will go

WHAT Object or thing

WHAT is the object - the thing or person receiving the action. It can be:

  • Noun - pizza, book, homework
  • Determiner + Noun - a book, the pizza
  • Object Pronoun - me, you, him, her, us, them

Examples: pizza, the keys, them, me

Note: When the object is a person, we use object pronouns (me, you, him, her, us, them) as the WHAT part. Example: "My friend helped me."

WHERE Place (usually after WHAT or at end)

WHERE tells us the place. It can be:

  • Preposition + Noun - at home, in the park
  • Preposition + Proper Noun - in Boston, at Starbucks
  • Adverb - here, there, everywhere

Examples: At the park, we played soccer. / We played soccer at the park.

WHEN Time (beginning or end of sentence)

WHEN tells us the time. It can be:

  • Time word - now, today, yesterday
  • Time phrase - every day, last night
  • Preposition + Time - at 3pm, on Monday

Examples: Yesterday, I studied English. / I studied English yesterday.

Standard order: She studied English at the library yesterday.

WHAT comes directly after the verb. WHEN comes at the end.

WHEN before WHO: Last night, we watched a movie at home.

(Always add a comma after a WHEN expression at the beginning.)

WHERE before WHO: On the bus, he listens to music every day.

(Always add a comma after a WHERE expression at the beginning.)

Both WHERE and WHEN before WHO: Tomorrow at the restaurant, I will meet my friend.
WHERE is optional: He cooked dinner last night. (No WHERE needed)
My sister works at a hospital in the city. (No WHEN needed)
WHAT can be a person (object pronoun): Yesterday, my friend helped me.

When the object is a person, use object pronouns (me, you, him, her, us, them) as WHAT.

Questions

Questions follow similar rules, but the order changes slightly:

1. Yes/No Questions: VERB comes before WHO

Statement: She studied English at the library last night.
Question: Did she study English at the library last night?

Pattern: VERB + WHO + VERB + WHAT + WHERE + WHEN

2. WH- Questions: Question word replaces WHO/WHAT/WHERE/WHEN and comes first

WHO question: Who did they speak with on the phone?
WHAT question: What did they play in the park this morning?
WHERE question: Where did he watch the movie last weekend?
WHEN question: When did you meet your friend at the caf√©?

Pattern: Question Word + VERB + WHO + VERB + (WHAT/WHERE/WHEN)
